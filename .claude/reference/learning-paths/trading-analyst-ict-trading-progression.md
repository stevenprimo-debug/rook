# ICT Trading — Progression Path

## Who This Is For

The operator who wants to learn discretionary price-action trading using the Inner Circle Trader (ICT) framework — market structure, liquidity, order blocks, fair value gaps, killzones — and graduate from "watching charts" to a documented edge with sized risk. This path assumes you can lose money before you make money and that screen-time substitutes for tuition.

By the end of this path you should be able to:
- Mark up a chart with a named setup and define the invalidation.
- Size the position to 1% of book before pulling the trigger.
- Journal the trade with enough rigor to spot your own pattern leaks.
- Backtest a setup over 100 occurrences and read the expectancy honestly.
- Calibrate setups to current macro regime instead of fighting it.
- Survive a drawdown without revenge-trading.

## Stage 1: Foundations (weeks 1-4)

**Goal:** Speak the ICT vocabulary without flinching. Mark up any chart with structure, liquidity pools, order blocks, and fair value gaps. Understand killzones and session theory.

**Read / Watch:**
- **The Inner Circle Trader YouTube channel** — start with the "ICT Mentorship 2022" playlist, then the "ICT 2024 Mentorship" series. Free. The original source; everyone teaching this material elsewhere is derivative.
- **ICT Twitter / X account** (@InnerCircleTraderOfficial) — annotated charts in real time. Follow but do not paper-trade off live calls; observe pattern.
- The vendored reference clipping `context/references/ict-trading-ultimate-guide.md` — the consolidated vocabulary primer. Read first.
- *Trading in the Zone* — Mark Douglas. The psychology layer that prevents you from blowing up. Not ICT-specific but mandatory.
- *Technical Analysis of the Financial Markets* — John Murphy. Encyclopedia reference; skim chapters on candlesticks, support / resistance, volume.
- *Japanese Candlestick Charting Techniques* — the operator Nison. The foundational candlestick text; useful for the pre-ICT vocabulary.
- *The Disciplined Trader* — Mark Douglas (the precursor to *Trading in the Zone*).

**Practice:**
- Open TradingView (free tier sufficient). Pick one instrument — ES futures or EURUSD — and mark up the daily chart for the past 20 trading days.
- Annotate every higher-high, higher-low, break of structure, equal high, equal low, and obvious liquidity sweep.
- Replay the past five trading days on the 15-minute chart using TradingView's Bar Replay. Mark up in real time as if you did not know what comes next. Save annotated screenshots.
- Build a vocabulary glossary in your trading journal: BOS, CHoCH, OB, FVG, liquidity, killzone, session theory, premium / discount, draw on liquidity. One sentence each in your own words.
- Watch one full ICT session live (London or NY open) without trading. Just observe how price moves through liquidity. Take screenshots at every named ICT move.
- Build a "session-template" markup checklist: PD arrays, asian range, london high/low, NY open, NY judas swing. Use it on every chart.

**Skill check:**
- Hand someone a blank chart of ES futures from a week ago, no annotations. You can mark structure, three liquidity pools, two order blocks, and one fair value gap inside ten minutes — and explain why each matters for the next directional move.
- You can define each ICT term in your own words without leaning on the YouTube vocabulary.
- You can identify the current market structure (bullish, bearish, ranging) on three timeframes for the same instrument without contradicting yourself.

## Stage 2: Applied Practice (weeks 5-12)

**Goal:** Backtest one named setup 100 times. Develop a one-page trading plan. Run a paper-trading account with the discipline of a live account.

**Read / Watch:**
- ICT's **"2022 Mentorship: Daily Bias"** episodes and **"London Killzone"** episodes. Watch each three times. The third pass is where the structure actually clicks.
- **Cred (TheTradingGeek) YouTube channel** — applies ICT concepts to live ES / NQ futures. Useful for seeing the framework work on instruments other than forex.
- **MMXM YouTube channel** — order flow and market-maker model overlay; advanced ICT.
- *Reminiscences of a Stock Operator* — Edwin Lefèvre. The Jesse Livermore biographical novel. Trading psychology unchanged since 1923.
- The vendored reference `context/references/7-skills-quant-trading-firms.md` — discipline benchmarks from prop trading.

**Practice:**
- Pick ONE setup (e.g., London session fair-value-gap retrace into NY open).
- Backtest 100 occurrences manually on Bar Replay.
- Spreadsheet every trade: date, time, entry, stop, target, R-multiple, outcome, screenshot, notes.
- Write a one-page trading plan: instrument, session, setup criteria, invalidation rule, position sizing formula (1% of account / stop distance), max trades per day, hard stop on daily loss.
- Open a paper-trading account on **TradingView Paper Trading** or **NinjaTrader Sim** or **Tradovate Demo**.
- Trade your plan for 30 sessions. Do not deviate.
- Journal every trade. Take screenshots before entry, at management, at exit. Re-review the journal every Sunday.
- Build a daily routine: pre-market chart prep (15 min), session screen-time, post-session journal entry. The routine is the edge.
- Track your "session adherence" score weekly — what % of sessions did you trade your plan vs. deviate. The score predicts future P&L.

**Skill check:**
- Your backtest shows the setup's expectancy is positive over 100 trades.
- Your paper-trading equity curve matches the backtest within statistical noise.
- Your journal flags at least three personal pattern leaks you would not have caught without it.
- You can name your max-loss day in dollars and the day it happened — the discipline of facing the number matters.

## Stage 3: Advanced Mastery (months 4-12)

**Goal:** Go live with small size. Build a second uncorrelated setup. Develop posture-current macro overlay. Survive the first 100 live trades.

**Read / Watch:**
- ICT's **"Macro to Micro Top-Down Analysis"** mentorship modules. The framework for not getting chopped up in counter-trend sessions.
- **Stevenson YouTube channel** — ICT applied with macro overlay; one of the few derivative teachers who adds value.
- *Market Wizards* and *The New Market Wizards* — Jack Schwager. Interview format; the lessons compound across chapters.
- *Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard* — Mark Minervini. Different framework (CAN SLIM-adjacent) but the risk discipline transfers.
- *The Disciplined Trader* — Mark Douglas. Sequel to *Trading in the Zone*; deeper on the cognitive trap of revenge trading.
- *Hedge Fund Market Wizards* — Jack Schwager (specifically the Jaffray Woodriff and Jamie Mai chapters).

**Practice:**
- Fund a live account with capital you can afford to lose entirely.
- Trade your plan at 1/10th your target size for the first 50 trades. Do not increase size until expectancy is verified live.
- Build a second uncorrelated setup. Backtest it 100 times. Add it to the plan only when it does not interfere with setup one's screen-time discipline.
- Add a daily macro brief to your routine: DXY direction, US10Y direction, key economic releases, FOMC week vs. non-FOMC week.
- The setup that worked in 2021 does not work the same in 2026; the macro overlay is the calibration.
- Quarterly review: equity curve, win rate, average R, biggest drawdown, behavioral notes.
- Compare your live results to the backtest. The gap between them is your tuition bill — pay it consciously.
- Develop a kill-switch protocol: at X% drawdown you stop trading and reset. Define X. Stick to it.
- Build a post-trade tape-review discipline: every Sunday review every trade with the chart open. Find the pattern the journal hasn't named yet.

**Skill check:**
- Over a 100-live-trade rolling window your realized expectancy is positive.
- Your max drawdown stayed inside the plan's risk limit.
- You can explain three trades you took that you should NOT have (the journal caught them).
- Your second setup is uncorrelated to your first — they don't both trigger and miss on the same day.
- You can defend the current macro posture in two sentences: trend bias, risk-on / risk-off, key data this week.

## Ongoing Development

**Stay current:**
- **The Inner Circle Trader YouTube channel** — new mentorship cycles drop yearly; the framework evolves.
- **The Macro Trading Floor podcast** — Alfonso Peccatiello. Macro overlay that informs daily bias.
- **The Forward Guidance podcast** — Jack Farley (Blockworks). Macro-rate environment context.
- **TradingView ideas feed** — filter to ICT-tagged authors; observe annotations, not signals.
- **Bloomberg Surveillance** podcast — daily macro discipline.
- **Real Vision** — long-form macro and trader interviews.
- **Chat With Traders podcast** — interviews with prop traders and quants; the discipline-and-mindset content is highest signal.
- **The CFA Institute's Enterprising Investor** blog — for the institutional-side discipline.
- **The CME Group's daily commentary** — for futures-specific context.
- **TradingView's weekly digest** of top community ideas — useful for cross-checking your own bias.
- **Hedgeye's daily macro brief** — opinionated; useful even when wrong.
- **The Daily Shot newsletter** — daily charts on global macro; tier-1 data visualizations.
- **The Inner Circle Trader's official documentation site** — when published, the canonical text of the framework.
- **The Stock Twits feed for your traded ticker** — read for sentiment-as-fade-signal, not as trade idea.
- **Bookmap heatmap demos** — order-flow visualization that pairs with ICT's liquidity language.
- **Sierra Chart and NinjaTrader webinars** — for the platform-side discipline if you trade futures.
- **Quantitative Tools by Tom Basso (interview archive)** — for the systematic-trader perspective that complements discretionary work.
- **The Schwab Network and CME's Open Markets** — daily market-open coverage; useful for cross-checking your session bias.

**Communities to join:**
- The **ICT Discord servers** that surface annually around each mentorship — quality varies; observe for two weeks before posting.
- **Elite Trader** forums (futures section) — old-school, high signal-to-noise on tape reading.
- **Trade2Win** forums — international audience; useful for cross-session perspective.
- A small accountability group (3-5 traders max) where you post one trade per day with thesis and outcome. Group of strangers > Twitter alpha.
- **The CMT Association** (Chartered Market Technician) — if you want to formalize the technical-analysis side of the discipline.

**Quarterly cadence:**
- Print your equity curve. Print your trade journal. Sit down for two hours and review.
- Write one paragraph to next-quarter you about what to stop doing.
- Re-backtest your live setup against the most recent 100 trades; compare expectancy to your original backtest.
- Audit your "session adherence" score for the quarter. If it dropped, ask why.
