# Prospecting Discipline — Progression Path

## Who This Is For

The operator who builds the lists that feed the outreach engine — pulling contacts from [your prospecting tool], Apollo, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Clay, or equivalents; scoring against ICP; ranking by observable buying signal — and refusing to confuse "I uploaded 5,000 records" with "I sourced 50 actually-in-market leads." Founders, SDRs, sales operators, RevOps leads.

By the end of this path you should be able to:
- Define an ICP with named filters.
- Hunt observable buying signals at scale.
- Build a list sized to the cadence math.
- Refresh the list before staleness collapses the conversion rate.
- Develop a multi-source enrichment pipeline.
- Score leads with a confidence-driven priority rank.

## Stage 1: Foundations (weeks 1-3)

**Goal:** Internalize ICP definition. Build your first list from named filters. Develop signal-vs-demographic instinct.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Predictable Revenue* — Aaron Ross. The original SDR / list-building playbook; dated tactics, intact structural model.
- *Fanatical Prospecting* — Jeb Blount. The activity-math chapters are mandatory; the list-quality chapters are the keepers.
- *Outbound Sales, No Fluff* — Rex Biberston and Ryan Reisert. The modern tactical update.
- *Hacking Sales* — Max Altschuler. The data-and-tooling lens on prospecting; the framework section is what holds.
- **Sales Hacker** (saleshacker.com) — the prospecting tag archive.
- **Apollo Academy** (apollo.io/academy) — free; product-tied but genuinely useful even if you use a different tool.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator's official training** — free with the tool; the filter-craft is the discipline.
- The vendored reference clipping `context/references/hubspot-academy.md` and `hubspot-academy-1.md` for CRM and list-management fundamentals.

**Practice:**
- Define your ICP in writing: industry / sub-industry, employee count range, revenue range, tech stack signals, geography, role title, role function, role seniority, persona / buying-committee position. Every dimension named. Every dimension defended.
- Build one list of 100 contacts using only ICP filters (no signal yet). Pressure-test against an actual rep or operator who works that segment — they should agree 80%+ of the list is in-spec.
- Develop a signal-vs-demographic instinct: for one company, list 5 demographic facts (size, vertical, geography, age) and 5 observable signals (recent hire, recent funding, recent product launch, recent content published, recent tech-stack change). The signals are the prospecting asset; the demographics are the filter.

**Skill check:**
- Your ICP definition fits on one page.
- Your peer reviewer can read it and predict which companies are in / out.
- Your 100-contact list is 80%+ in-spec.
- You can defend each filter dimension with a reason tied to past won-deal patterns.

## Stage 2: Applied Practice (weeks 4-10)

**Goal:** Hunt buying signals. Score and rank a list. Run a list-refresh cadence.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Combo Prospecting* — Tony Hughes. The multi-touch, multi-channel prospecting frame; the chapters on triggering events are the keepers.
- *The Sales Development Playbook* — Trish Bertuzzi. The full SDR operating manual; the persona-and-segment chapters carry to list design.
- **Clay's content** (clay.com/blog) — the modern data-enrichment platform's blog; the playbooks on signal hunting are the densest available.
- **Apollo's "Sales Triggers"** — free content on event-based prospecting.
- **Lavender.ai's content** — for the email side; useful when scoring which signals actually convert.
- **6sense / Bombora content on intent data** — the B2B-intent-data primer; free reports.
- **Cognism's blog** on European prospecting — useful counterweight to US-centric playbooks.
- **G2 and Capterra** — useful (and underused) prospect-side review-mining grounds.

**Practice:**
- Build a signal library: 10 observable signal types you'll hunt, the data source for each (LinkedIn job-change, Crunchbase funding, BuiltWith tech stack, GA-data on traffic, job-posting changes on Greenhouse / Lever, podcast appearances, content publishing). Document the source and the freshness window.
- Build a scoring rubric: ICP-fit score (0-10), signal-density score (number of fresh signals: 0-5), role-priority score (champion / influencer / blocker / unknown). Combined score = priority rank. Apply to one list of 300 contacts.
- Run a list-refresh cycle: 60-day cadence on a real list. Track which contacts went stale (changed jobs, company changed status, signal expired). Remove or update. The discipline of refresh is the asset.
- Run one experiment on signal-hunting: pick one platform you haven't used (Clay, Ocean.io, Surfe, Common Room, Champify, UserGems). Run it for 30 days. Measure delta on list quality.

**Skill check:**
- Your scored list of 300 contacts produced a top-50 cohort that converted to meetings at a measurably-higher rate than the bottom-50.
- Your list-refresh cadence is on a 60-day cycle and removing stale contacts before they hurt the cadence stats.
- Your signal library has at least 10 named signal types with documented data sources.

## Stage 3: Advanced Mastery (months 3-9)

**Goal:** Account-based list architecture. Build a multi-source enrichment pipeline. Develop signal-velocity instinct.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Account-Based Marketing* — Chris Golec, Peter Isaacson, Jessica Fewless. The ABM operating manual; the persona-mapping chapters apply to list design.
- *No Forms. No Spam. No Cold Calls.* — Latane Conant. The 6sense CRO's ABM-modern manifesto; the tactical detail is current.
- *Hyper-Connected Selling* — David Fisher. The signal-hunting through LinkedIn discipline.
- *The Revenue Acceleration Rules* — Shashi Upadhyay (Lattice Engines / 6sense). The intent-data and prospect-scoring discipline at depth.
- **Common Room's playbooks** — the modern community-led-signal-hunting platform; free content is high signal.
- **Champify's content** on champion-tracking — the "follow champions to new companies" play, which is one of the highest-converting modern signals.
- **UserGems' content** on champion alerts.
- **Apollo / Outreach / Clay engineering blogs** — for the tooling-side discipline.
- **The Pavilion CRO-track and RevOps-track content** (paid).
- **6sense, Bombora, Demandbase, Madison Logic** — the intent-data ecosystem's white papers and reports.
- **Sangram Vajre's books and FlipMyFunnel content** — the ABM-thought-leadership archive.

**Practice:**
- Build an account-based list: 50 named target accounts, 5-10 contacts per account, multi-threaded (champion + economic buyer + decision-maker + influencer + technical evaluator). Annotate each contact with their role in the buying committee.
- Build a multi-source enrichment pipeline: starting list (Apollo / [your prospecting tool]) → enrichment (Clay / Surfe) → signal layer (Champify / UserGems / Common Room) → CRM. Document the data flow. Test the pipeline on 100 contacts. Measure data freshness and accuracy.
- Develop signal-velocity instinct: which signals are leading (predict in-market in 30+ days), which are concurrent (in-market now), which are lagging (already engaged with competitor). Tag every signal type in your library by velocity.
- Run a quarterly list-architecture review: total addressable market estimate, list coverage, list freshness, list-to-pipeline conversion. The review surfaces whether the list is the bottleneck or the cadence is.

**Skill check:**
- Your account-based list produced multi-threaded engagement on multiple named accounts.
- Your enrichment pipeline is operating at scale without you manually scrubbing each record.
- You can name which signal-types convert at what rates in your domain.
- Your TAM (total addressable market) estimate is sized and the list-coverage ratio is measured.

## Ongoing Development

**Stay current:**
- **Clay's blog** — weekly.
- **Apollo's blog and Academy** — weekly.
- **Sales Hacker** — daily.
- **6sense and Bombora reports** — quarterly.
- **The 30 Minutes to President's Club podcast** — daily tactics; the prospecting-tagged episodes.
- **GTM Partners' content** (gtmpartners.com).
- **Pavilion** — newsletter and content.

**Communities to join:**
- **RevGenius** — free Slack; sales and revenue community.
- **Pavilion** — paid; the RevOps and SDR-leader tracks are the most relevant.
- **Wizards of Ops** — RevOps community; free.
- **Clay's community** — free; tool-tied but useful for signal-hunting craft.
- A small peer-prospector circle (3-5 SDRs / list-builders) — monthly swap of working signal types and dead signal types.

**Quarterly cadence:**
- Pull your list data, your meeting data, your pipeline data.
- Trace the conversion: which signals converted, which didn't.
- Update the signal library. Rebuild the next quarter's lists from what worked.
- Audit the ICP definition: which filters held; which let in noise; tighten or loosen.
- Score the enrichment pipeline: data accuracy, data freshness, cost per enriched record.
- Identify the one segment or persona that converted disproportionately well; build the next list around it.
