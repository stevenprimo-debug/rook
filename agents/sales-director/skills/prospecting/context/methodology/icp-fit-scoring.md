# ICP-Fit Scoring

## What This Framework Is

ICP-fit scoring is the discipline of systematically rating each
prospect against the Ideal Customer Profile criteria before
committing outreach or pipeline resources. The framework holds
that **prospect quality compounds** — pursuing well-fit
prospects produces higher response rates, larger deal sizes,
shorter sales cycles, and stronger references; pursuing poorly-fit
prospects produces wasted outreach, low close rates, and
under-margin deals.

The ICP is the named profile of the prospects most likely to
benefit from the offering. For the operator's [your employer] work (per locked
memory), the ICP spans:

- **Wide-net coverage**: enterprise down to 200-employee
  mid-market.
- **Vertical breadth**: ALL verticals — law firms, accounting,
  engineering, higher ed, healthcare, hospitality, real
  estate, corporate enterprise.
- **Right contacts**: VP Workplace Tech, Dir Facilities, Dir
  Corp Real Estate, CTO/CIO.
- **Right scope**: $500K-$8M+ permanent experiential/immersive,
  LED-dominant, multi-zone.

ICP-fit scoring rates each prospect against multiple dimensions
and produces a composite score that gates pipeline entry.
High-fit prospects enter the pipeline; medium-fit get more
research; low-fit get parked or dropped.

The framework operates as a recurring discipline because the
ICP is not static — as the operator's product mix evolves, as market
conditions shift, as new wedges open, the ICP refines. Today's
ICP is a working hypothesis; tomorrow's ICP incorporates this
week's learning.

## Why It Matters For This Agent

Prospecting Agent's bench gates on three principles: ICP-Fit-Pole,
Signal-Density-Pole, and Research-Compounding-Pole. The framework
is the operating implementation of the first pole.

- **ICP-Fit-Pole** asks: "Does this prospect actually fit the
  ICP, or are we adding them because they're easy to find?"
  The framework's scoring rubric is the gate.

For the operator's [your employer] outreach pipeline, ICP-fit scoring is what
separates the prospects that produce deals from the prospects
that produce activity-without-revenue. Without the discipline,
prospecting drifts toward what's findable (easy to research on
[your prospecting tool]) rather than what's high-fit (the right intersection
of size, vertical, contact tier, and triggerability).

## Core Concepts

### 1. The Multi-Dimensional Scoring Rubric

Each prospect gets scored on multiple dimensions:

**Organization size:**
- 5/5 — enterprise with named major facility.
- 4/5 — Fortune 1000 or large privately-held with comparable
  facility scale.
- 3/5 — Mid-market 1,000-5,000 employee with significant facility
  footprint.
- 2/5 — 200-1,000 employee with named facility need.
- 1/5 — <200 employee.

**Vertical fit:**
- 5/5 — Verticals with proven [your employer] track record (law,
  accounting, financial services, headquarters/RTO scenarios).
- 4/5 — Verticals with adjacent fit (healthcare admin,
  higher-ed administration, hospitality corporate, professional
  services).
- 3/5 — Verticals with feasibility but limited reference base
  (manufacturing HQ, government, non-profits).
- 2/5 — Verticals with structural mismatch (retail consumer,
  pure e-commerce).

**Contact tier:**
- 5/5 — VP Workplace Tech, Dir Corp Real Estate, CTO/CIO at
  named org.
- 4/5 — Dir Facilities, Dir IT (when facility-overseeing), Sr
  VP Operations.
- 3/5 — Manager-level in target functions; may need escalation.
- 2/5 — Individual contributor; long path to decision authority.

**Trigger event presence:**
- 5/5 — Recent trigger (new construction, executive move, RTO
  mandate, acquisition, IPO) within 30-60 days.
- 4/5 — Trigger within 60-180 days.
- 3/5 — Anticipated trigger (lease event, growth announcement)
  in pipeline.
- 2/5 — No specific trigger; cold approach.

**Project scope fit:**
- 5/5 — Verifiable need for $500K-$8M+ permanent integration
  with LED-dominant or multi-zone scope.
- 4/5 — Likely need for $100K-$500K integration scope.
- 3/5 — Possible smaller-scope work.
- 2/5 — Scope below auto-reject thresholds.

**Composite score** = sum of dimensions. 25/25 is theoretical
maximum; 17+/25 is pipeline-priority; 13-16 is research-deeper;
<13 is park or drop.

### 2. The Wide-Net ICP

Per locked memory, the ICP is intentionally wide:

> Wide net ICP: enterprise down to 200-employee mid-market
> across ALL verticals — law firms, accounting, engineering,
> higher ed, healthcare, hospitality, real estate, corporate
> enterprise.

This breadth exists because the [your employer] offering (permanent
experiential/immersive AV) applies across vertical boundaries.
A 240-seat town hall at a law firm is structurally similar to
a 240-seat town hall at a corporate HQ — both need the same
zone treatments.

The wide-net ICP is the prospecting pool. ICP-fit scoring is
what triages within the pool — not every prospect in the pool
is high-fit at any given moment.

### 3. The Tier-Stratified Outreach Mapping

ICP-fit scores map to outreach tiers (per the locked [your employer]
outreach pipeline):

- **17+/25 with C-Suite contact** → Tier 1 outreach
  (deepest research, most specific message, longest cycle
  investment).
- **17+/25 with VP-level contact** → Tier 2 outreach
  (high specificity, named circumstance, named outcome).
- **13-16/25** → Tier 3 outreach (moderate specificity, possibly
  longer warm-up via content engagement first).
- **<13/25** → Tier 4 if any value, otherwise park.

The mapping ensures research investment matches expected return.
High-fit prospects get high-investment outreach; lower-fit
prospects get appropriate-investment outreach.

### 4. The Signal-Density Multiplier

Beyond raw ICP-fit, signal density (how strong the buying
signal is) multiplies the score. Per the companion
signal-density-discipline:

- **Multiple recent triggers** at the same organization
  multiply the score (executive move + new construction +
  RTO announcement = much stronger fit than any one signal).
- **Single trigger** at moderate fit may still warrant
  outreach if the trigger is strong (e.g., new facilities VP
  who publicly mentioned AV strategy).

Signal density compounds with ICP-fit. A 19/25 prospect with
strong signal density beats a 22/25 prospect with no signal
density — because the strong-signal prospect is more likely
to engage right now.

### 5. The Research Triage

ICP-fit scoring gates research investment:

- **17+ score**: deep account brief — facility footprint,
  named decision-makers (multiple), recent triggers (multiple),
  named pain points, fit assessment with specific named scope.
- **13-16 score**: standard account brief — named target,
  named role, named organization, recent trigger if any, fit
  hypothesis.
- **<13 score**: minimum verification — confirm role, confirm
  email, no deeper research.

The triage prevents research-budget waste on low-fit prospects.

### 6. The Compounding Research Asset

ICP-fit research compounds across cycles:

- **Account brief** from earlier prospecting is reusable when
  triggers later fire at the same account.
- **Disqualification reasons** at one prospect inform pattern
  recognition at similar prospects.
- **High-fit pattern signatures** (named features of the
  highest-fit prospects) refine the ICP itself.

The discipline: research outputs are captured in a structured
form that future cycles can reference. Per the locked
compounding-append memory pattern, account knowledge
accumulates over time.

### 7. The ICP Refinement Cycle

The ICP is hypothesis, not law. Periodically:

- **Win patterns** — which prospect profiles produced the
  closed deals? Did the ICP predict them?
- **Loss patterns** — which prospect profiles produced
  rejections or stalls? Did the ICP flag them?
- **Surprise wins** — deals from prospects the ICP rated
  low. What did they have in common?
- **Surprise losses** — high-ICP prospects who didn't engage.
  What did they have in common?

ICP refinement happens quarterly or after significant pattern
shifts. The compounding learning produces better targeting
over time.

## Common Applications

**Weekly prospecting batch:**
The agent runs the locked [your employer] outreach pipeline. [your prospecting tool]
search produces candidate prospects. Each gets ICP-fit scored.
Score >17 with C-Suite contact routes to T1; >17 with VP
routes to T2; 13-16 routes to T3; <13 parks or drops. Tier
assignment drives message template and research depth.

**Account-brief generation for high-fit prospect:**
A 22/25 prospect with new construction trigger gets the deep
brief: facility footprint, named decision-makers (VP Workplace
Tech, Dir Facilities, Dir Corp Real Estate, CTO), recent
triggers, named spaces in the new construction, named pain
indicators, fit assessment with specific scope hypothesis.

**Disqualification documentation:**
A prospect scores 8/25 (small org, weak contact, no trigger,
ambiguous scope). Disqualified. The disqualification reason
gets logged: "below scale floor, no trigger present." Pattern
contributes to ICP refinement — multiple similar
disqualifications may indicate the ICP needs sharpening.

**Trigger-event prospecting:**
The agent monitors trigger events (executive moves, construction
announcements, RTO mandates) across the wide-net ICP. When a
trigger fires at a previously-low-fit prospect, ICP-fit re-scores
— the trigger may push the score from 12 to 17, justifying
fresh outreach.

**Quarterly ICP refinement:**
The agent reviews the last quarter's wins, losses, surprises.
Patterns refine the scoring rubric. For example, if multiple
wins came from a vertical that scored only 3/5 on vertical
fit, the rubric may need updating.

**Per locked memory: [your prospecting tool] MCP wired up.** The agent uses
[your prospecting tool] via the MCP connector for prospect data. Direct
search, enrichment, and recommendation calls produce the
prospect candidate pool.

**Per locked memory: outreach tracker.** ICP-fit scores and
disqualification reasons get logged to the outreach tracker.
The tracker compounds account knowledge across cycles.

## Anti-patterns (when this framework is misapplied)

**Easy-to-find prospecting.** Adding prospects because they
were easy to surface in [your prospecting tool], not because they fit the
ICP. Pipeline fills with low-fit prospects; outreach response
rates collapse.

**Volume over fit.** Treating prospecting as a volume metric.
"We added 200 prospects this week" — but if the prospects
don't fit, the activity doesn't produce revenue.

**Per locked feedback: auto-reject thresholds.** Prospects
whose realistic scope is below $100K value, <15% GP, <$15K
commission, <$300/hr efficiency don't enter the pipeline —
regardless of ICP-fit score on other dimensions.

**ICP rigidity.** Treating the ICP as fixed rather than as
hypothesis. Misses opportunities the rubric doesn't yet
recognize. The quarterly refinement cycle is the prevention.

**Vertical exclusion at ICP level.** Treating any vertical as
"not for [your employer]" based on assumption rather than evidence. The
wide-net ICP exists because vertical fit is contextual.

**Per locked feedback: "Verify Project Status Before Speaking."**
ICP-fit scoring uses verified prospect data, not assumed data.
Stale information produces stale scores.

**Tier inflation.** Treating every prospect as T1 because
"they might be high-fit." Tier discipline gates research
investment; inflation produces research-budget waste.

**Research without compounding capture.** Doing the account
research, then losing it in inbox or working memory rather
than capturing it in a structured form. The next cycle
re-does the research from scratch.

**Per locked feedback: "Don't Infer Client Entity Names."**
ICP-fit research uses the exact names the customer uses for
facilities, roles, spaces. Inferred names produce errors that
erode trust at outreach stage.

**Trigger-blind prospecting.** Ignoring trigger events because
"we don't have time to monitor them." Triggers multiply
response rates 3-5x; trigger-blind prospecting leaves the
multiplier on the table.

**Per locked feedback: "Filter — Personal-Tool Patterns vs
Agent-Team Patterns."** ICP-fit data must be team-readable.
Personal-shorthand notes that only the prospecting agent
understands fail when sales-director or outreach-agent need
to read them.
