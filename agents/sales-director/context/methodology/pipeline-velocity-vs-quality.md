# Pipeline Velocity vs. Quality

## What This Framework Is

Pipeline velocity vs. quality is the discipline that gates every
sales decision on the trade-off between **moving more deals
through the pipeline faster** (velocity) and **moving the right
deals through with appropriate qualification rigor** (quality).
The framework holds that **these are not opposed in the long
run, only in the short run** — and that sales directors who
optimize purely for velocity produce closed deals that fail at
delivery, while sales directors who optimize purely for quality
produce pipelines that starve revenue.

The framework operates across the pipeline lifecycle:

- **Top of funnel** — prospecting decisions (volume vs.
  fit-targeted).
- **Qualification** — pursuing decisions (velocity to close vs.
  qualification rigor).
- **Proposal stage** — competitive decisions (win-rate vs.
  margin).
- **Negotiation** — concession decisions (close-now vs.
  protect-margins).
- **Close** — commitment decisions (predictable wins vs.
  stretch opportunities).

Each stage has the velocity-quality trade-off in different form.
The sales director's job is to manage the trade-off explicitly
at each stage, not silently default to one or the other.

For the operator's [your employer] work (per locked thresholds):
- **Auto-reject** thresholds (quality floor): <$100K value,
  <15% GP, <$15K commission, <$300/hr efficiency.
- **Priority targets** (quality bias): $500K-$8M+ permanent
  experiential/immersive, LED-dominant, multi-zone.
- **Right contacts** (qualification rigor): VP Workplace Tech,
  Dir Facilities, Dir Corp Real Estate, CTO/CIO.

The thresholds embed the quality floor. Velocity above the
floor is encouraged; velocity below the floor is the failure
mode.

## Why It Matters For This Agent

Sales Director's bench gates on three principles: Pipeline-
Velocity-Pole, Customer-Truth-Pole, and Margin-Protection-Pole.
The framework is the operating implementation of the first and
third poles.

- **Pipeline-Velocity-Pole** asks: "Is the pipeline moving fast
  enough to hit the $5M annual target / $9-10M stretch?" The
  framework's velocity discipline is the gate.

- **Margin-Protection-Pole** asks: "Are we accepting deals that
  protect commission floor and gross margin?" The framework's
  quality discipline is the gate.

For the operator's [your employer] income-bridge work, the framework prevents two
failure modes:

1. **Velocity trap** — chasing every opportunity to keep pipeline
   numbers up, accepting deals below the quality floor, burning
   delivery time on low-margin work.

2. **Quality trap** — over-qualifying every prospect, slowing
   pipeline to a crawl, missing revenue targets while the
   "perfect" pipeline doesn't close fast enough.

## Core Concepts

### 1. The Pipeline Tier System

Pipeline opportunities are tiered by quality + velocity profile:

**Tier 1 — Priority targets (high quality, varied velocity):**
- $500K-$8M+ permanent experiential/immersive.
- LED-dominant, multi-zone.
- VP-level contacts.
- Auto-pursue; velocity matters; rigor in proposal.

**Tier 2 — Standard targets (medium quality, expected velocity):**
- $100K-$500K standard integrations.
- Single-zone or simpler scope.
- Director-level contacts.
- Pursue; standard pipeline pace.

**Tier 3 — Lower-priority (medium quality, slow velocity):**
- $100K-$300K simpler projects.
- Lower complexity.
- May come with longer cycle times.
- Pursue if pipeline depth justifies.

**Auto-reject** (quality floor failure):
- <$100K value.
- <15% GP.
- <$15K commission.
- <$300/hr efficiency.

The tier system makes triage explicit. Every incoming opportunity
gets tiered; velocity expectations match the tier.

### 2. The Qualification Rigor Calibration

Qualification rigor calibrates to tier:

**Tier 1 qualification:**
- Confirm the named decision-maker (VP-level, named role).
- Verify project scope (sq ft, zones, scope of work).
- Confirm timeline and budget commitment.
- Confirm decision process and competing vendors.
- Confirm fit with [your employer] capability profile.

**Tier 2 qualification:**
- Confirm decision-maker level (director or above).
- Verify scope at sufficient detail for proposal.
- Confirm timeline (approximate, refined in proposal).
- Confirm budget range.

**Tier 3 qualification:**
- Verify scope is real (not fishing for estimates).
- Confirm decision-maker contact.
- Time-box qualification to avoid sink.

The rigor protects against the failure mode where every prospect
gets the same qualification depth — wasting deep qualification
on Tier 3 opportunities, or rushing Tier 1 opportunities through
shallow qualification.

### 3. The Win-Rate Reality

Win rates differ by stage and tier. Honest tracking:

- **Top of funnel → qualified opportunity**: typical 10-30% pass
  rate, depending on prospecting quality.
- **Qualified → proposal**: typical 50-70% (qualification removed
  the worst-fit prospects).
- **Proposal → close**: typical 25-45% (competitive stage).
- **Tier 1 wins outperform Tier 3 wins** in win rate when
  qualification is done right.

The discipline: tracking actual win rates, not assumed ones. The
pipeline projection multiplies stage-by-stage win rates against
opportunity values to project realistic closed revenue. Pipelines
that don't track win rates produce optimistic projections that
miss targets.

### 4. The Velocity Bottleneck Diagnostic

When pipeline velocity is below target, the diagnostic identifies
the bottleneck:

- **Top of funnel light** — not enough prospecting; not enough
  qualified opportunities entering. Fix: increase prospecting
  volume or improve targeting (per the prospecting-agent's ICP-fit
  scoring).
- **Qualification drag** — too many opportunities stuck in
  qualification. Fix: improve qualification framework, time-box
  qualifications, disqualify faster.
- **Proposal cycle drag** — proposals take too long to produce.
  Fix: template improvements (per [example enterprise customer] v4 master template),
  parallel work, dedicated proposal time.
- **Close drag** — proposals stall in negotiation or decision.
  Fix: surface objections earlier, qualify decision process,
  follow up with discipline.

Each bottleneck has different remediation. The diagnostic surfaces
which is binding.

### 5. The Margin Discipline at Negotiation

At negotiation, the temptation is to concede on price/margin to
close. The discipline:

- **Floor below auto-reject thresholds = walk away**. No deal is
  worth the floor breach.
- **Above floor, concede on terms first, price last**. Payment
  terms, schedule flexibility, scope adjustments — all
  preferable to price cuts that compound on the GP percentage.
- **Concession-for-concession**. Every concession matches a
  client concession (e.g., faster payment terms, larger scope
  commitment).
- **Walk-away authority**. The sales director defines walk-away
  conditions in writing before negotiation starts. Walking
  away from below-threshold deals preserves margin discipline.

Per locked thresholds, the auto-reject floor exists for a reason:
deals below it produce activity without compounding wealth.
Conceding below the floor produces work that doesn't fund the
freedom fund.

### 6. The Forecast Honesty

Sales forecasts are honest about probability, not aspirational:

- **Commit forecast** — deals the director will close this period
  with very high confidence.
- **Likely forecast** — deals expected to close with reasonable
  confidence.
- **Stretch forecast** — deals possible but uncertain.

Each opportunity gets honest probability. Aggregated forecasts
reflect actual likelihood, not the director's optimism. Per
locked feedback ("Numbers-Honest-Pole" — cross-pole with
Finance Manager), forecasts that overstate cause downstream
financial planning failures.

### 7. The Pipeline Decay

Pipelines decay if not refreshed. The discipline:

- **Stale opportunities** (no activity in 30+ days) get reviewed:
  re-engage, escalate, or close-lost.
- **Stuck stages** get diagnosed: what's blocking the next
  movement?
- **Dead deals** get formally closed-lost so they stop
  inflating pipeline numbers.

Pipelines that don't decay-prune produce false health: "X
million in pipeline" includes deals that will never close,
suppressing the urgency to add fresh opportunities.

## Common Applications

**Weekly pipeline review:**
The agent runs the tiered review. For each opportunity: tier
classification, current stage, last activity, projected close
date, projected value, projected GP. Surfaces: bottlenecks,
stale opportunities, dead deals, gap to target. Output: action
list for the week.

**Tier 1 opportunity prioritization:**
A new $2M opportunity arrives at a enterprise facility. The
agent runs Tier 1 qualification rigor: confirms named decision-
maker, scope details, timeline, decision process, competitive
landscape. If qualified, opportunity gets prioritized pipeline
attention.

**Auto-reject on small deal:**
A $75K opportunity arrives. Below the $100K floor. Auto-reject.
The agent declines or refers to non-[your employer] channels. Time saved
goes to Tier 1 + Tier 2 work.

**Margin defense at negotiation:**
A Tier 1 deal at $1.5M is negotiating final terms. Client asks
for 5% price cut. The agent surfaces: 5% cut reduces GP from
22% to 17% (still above floor); commission impact is X; trade-off
options include payment terms, scope, schedule. The director
makes the call with the math visible.

**Forecast review with Finance Manager:**
End of quarter, the agent runs forecast review against
Finance Manager. Commit + likely forecasts are aggregated;
stretch is separate. Cross-pole with Numbers-Honest-Pole: are
forecasts honest? Aggregate matches what's actually likely to
close.

**Wealth-creator-mode escalation:**

## Anti-patterns (when this framework is misapplied)

**Velocity-only optimization.** Chasing every opportunity to
keep pipeline numbers up. Auto-reject floor gets breached;
low-margin work absorbs delivery capacity; commission income
underperforms vs. effort.

**Quality-only optimization.** Over-qualifying every prospect;
slowing pipeline to a crawl; missing $5M annual target while
the "perfect" pipeline drags.

**Per locked feedback: auto-reject thresholds.** <$100K value,
<15% GP, <$15K commission, <$300/hr efficiency = auto-reject.
Breaching these for "we need the win" produces work that
doesn't compound.

**Per locked feedback: priority target focus.** $500K-$8M+
permanent experiential/immersive. The bias toward these isn't
preference — it's where the commission and margin math works.

**Forecast optimism.** Forecasting what the director hopes will
close, not what's actually likely. Downstream planning fails
when forecasts miss; trust damage with Finance Manager and
the operator's planning process.

**Stale opportunity accumulation.** Pipelines with months of
dead deals inflate the numbers and suppress urgency. Decay-prune
discipline is the prevention.

**Per locked feedback: "Brand to the customer's trade."** Sales
outreach and proposals respect the client's trade. Generic
"transformative experiences" framing fails; specific operator-
trade language wins.

**Per locked feedback: "Client Data Publication Rule."** No
client data appears in public marketing, social posts, or
the Stack surfaces. Sales work and public-facing work have
strict separation.

**Per locked feedback: "Verify Project Status Before Speaking."**
Forecasting against memory of last week's state, not actual
current state. Honest forecasts require honest current data.

**Margin concession below threshold.** Conceding below the
auto-reject floor to "save the deal." The deal isn't worth
saving below the floor — it's worth walking away from.

**Per locked feedback: "Wealth Creator Mode."** Periodically
running the Naval-style stress test: is [your employer] income-bridge work
producing wealth, or just consuming time that mission products
need?
