# Pipeline Leadership — Progression Path

## Who This Is For

The operator who owns a sales pipeline — whether as VP Sales, head of revenue, or solo-founder running their own enterprise deals — and refuses to confuse "lots of meetings" with "predictable revenue." Founders selling B2B, sales leaders, agency principals, and account executives moving up.

By the end of this path you should be able to:
- Read a pipeline cold and forecast within 10%.
- Kill bad-fit deals fast.
- Qualify decision-maker access on every opp.
- Run a win-loss program that produces actual learnings.
- Coach reps from the deal-strategy seat instead of the closer seat.
- Build a quarterly business review (QBR) format that produces compounding insight.

## Stage 1: Foundations (weeks 1-3)

**Goal:** Internalize MEDDPICC (or equivalent qualification framework). Read a pipeline. Develop forecast discipline.

**Read / Watch:**
- *The Challenger Sale* — Matthew Dixon and Brent Adamson. The CEB research on what actually differentiates top reps; the teach-tailor-take-control thesis is the working model.
- *Spin Selling* — Neil Rackham. The Huthwaite research; the situation/problem/implication/need-payoff question structure is the discipline.
- *MEDDPICC: The Ultimate Guide* — Andy Whyte. The Force Management qualification framework that most enterprise SaaS sales orgs run on. The book is a quick read; the framework is the spine.
- *The Qualified Sales Leader* — John McMahon. The five-time CRO; the operating manual for running an enterprise sales org.
- **Force Management's MEDDPICC content** (forcemanagement.com) — free articles and webinars.
- **John McMahon on the 30 Minutes to President's Club podcast** — guest appearances; the operator detail compounds.
- *Predictable Revenue* — Aaron Ross. The original outbound SDR-to-AE playbook; dated in places, the structural model holds.

**Practice:**
- Pull a real pipeline (yours or one you've run). Score every open deal against MEDDPICC: Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Paper process, Identify pain, Champion, Competition. The deals that score <5 on the rubric should not be in your forecast.
- Run a deal-by-deal forecast cycle: commit, best case, pipeline, omitted. Forecast Friday for the close-Monday. Score yourself end-of-month on accuracy. Target: within 10%.
- Develop a kill-fast discipline: pick the three oldest deals in your pipeline. For each, write the kill-criterion. If criterion not met inside two weeks, kill. Most stuck deals are dead — calling them dead frees rep time for live deals.

**Skill check:**
- Your forecast accuracy is within 10% on the month.
- Your stale-deal count is dropping.
- You can read a peer's pipeline and identify the three deals most likely to slip.
- Every deal in your forecast has named champion, named economic buyer, and named decision criteria.

## Stage 2: Applied Practice (weeks 4-10)

**Goal:** Run a structured win-loss program. Coach a deal from the deal-strategy seat. Develop deal-quality instincts.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Mastering the Complex Sale* — Jeff Thull. The "diagnostic business development" framework; the discipline of "do not sell to a buyer who hasn't named their problem."
- *The Trusted Advisor* — David Maister, Charles Green, Robert Galford. The professional-services side of enterprise sales; the trust equation is the keeper.
- *Snap Selling* — Jill Konrath. The "simple-iNvaluable-aligned-Priorities" frame for selling to time-poor buyers.
- *New Sales. Simplified.* — Mike Weinberg. The hunter discipline; the email-and-call cadence that fills the pipeline.
- *To Sell Is Human* — Daniel Pink. The behavior-science layer; the ABC = Attune/Buoyancy/Clarity reframe.
- **30 Minutes to President's Club podcast** — Nick Cegelski and Armand Farrokh. The most concentrated tactical-sales-conversation archive currently in podcast form.
- **Pavilion (joinpavilion.com)** — sales-leader community; the content is free at the surface, paid for the depth.
- **Sales Hacker** (saleshacker.com) — daily articles; variable but useful pulse.
- The vendored reference clipping `context/references/hubspot-academy.md` and `hubspot-academy-1.md` for fundamental CRM and sales-process discipline.

**Practice:**
- Run a win-loss program: for the next 10 closed deals (won or lost), interview the buyer 30 days post-close. Use a structured template. Aggregate patterns over 10 interviews. The patterns are the playbook update.
- Coach one rep (or yourself in another mode) on one live deal from the deal-strategy seat. Use the MEDDPICC rubric. Identify the weakest dimension. Build the next step that strengthens it. Do not close the deal yourself.
- Develop a deal-review cadence: weekly 15-min deal reviews on the top 5 deals; monthly 60-min deep reviews on the top 3. The discipline is the asset, not the format.
- Build a churned-deal post-mortem: for every deal you lose this quarter, write a one-paragraph post-mortem. After 5+, the pattern emerges.

**Skill check:**
- Your win-loss program produced at least two actionable changes to the playbook.
- Your coached deal advanced (or got killed with clarity).
- Your forecast accuracy is improving month-over-month.
- Your deal-review cadence is running on time and producing decision-quality discussions.

## Stage 3: Advanced Mastery (months 3-12)

**Goal:** Build a sales org. Develop enterprise-sales muscle. Run a revenue operating system.

**Read / Watch:**
- *From Impossible to Inevitable* — Aaron Ross and Jason Lemkin. The hypergrowth playbook; the nail-a-niche / create-predictable-pipeline / make-sales-scalable chapters are the framework.
- *Sales Management. Simplified.* — Mike Weinberg. The non-fluff sales-leadership operating manual.
- *Cracking the Sales Management Code* — Jason Jordan and Michelle Vazzana. The metrics architecture for sales orgs.
- *The Sales Acceleration Formula* — Mark Roberge. The HubSpot CRO's hiring, training, ramping, comp model; the metrics-driven scale model.
- *Sell Different!* — Lee Salz. The differentiation discipline that converts a commoditized space.
- *Strategic Selling* — Robert Miller, Stephen Heiman, Tad Tuleja. The Miller Heiman blue sheet; the complex-deal political-mapping framework.
- *Mastering the Enterprise Sales Cycle* — for the long-deal patience and stakeholder-management discipline.
- *Multi-Threading* (concept; covered across MEDDPICC and Strategic Selling) — the discipline of selling to multiple champions and stakeholders in parallel.
- **Pavilion's CRO-track content** (paid).
- **The Bridge Group's State of SaaS Sales reports** (annual; free).
- **OpenView Partners' sales-org benchmarks** — annual SaaS benchmarks; free.
- **GTM Partners' content** (gtmpartners.com) — sales and marketing-org operating-system depth.

**Practice:**
- Build a quota-and-comp model for one rep position: ramp schedule, monthly quota progression, accelerators, SPIFs. Pressure-test against industry benchmarks (OpenView, Bridge Group). Hire (or model the hire of) one rep against the model.
- Run a quarterly business review (QBR) format: pipeline coverage, win-rate, average deal size, sales cycle length, segment performance. Identify the one move that compounds across all five metrics.
- Develop an enterprise-deal blueprint: stakeholder map, decision process, paper process, mutual-action-plan template, executive-alignment cadence. Run one real enterprise deal end-to-end with the blueprint.
- Build a revenue operating system: pipeline metrics dashboard, forecast cadence, win-loss program, coaching cadence, comp plan, hiring plan. Document the OS so a successor could run it.

**Skill check:**
- Your forecast accuracy is consistently within 5-10%.
- Your win-rate is climbing.
- Your sales cycle length is shortening.
- Your enterprise-deal blueprint produced at least one closed enterprise deal that the buyer's exec sponsor would publicly endorse.
- Your revenue operating system would survive your departure for 90 days without degradation.

**Communities to join:**
- **Pavilion** — paid; sales and marketing leaders; the highest-density community currently.
- **Revenue Collective** (now part of Pavilion).
- **SaaStr** — community and conference; the founder-CRO overlap is high.
- **GTMNow / Sales Hacker Slack** — free; variable.
- A small peer-sales-leader circle (3-5 leaders) — monthly pipeline-review swaps with structured peer review.

**Quarterly cadence:**
- Pull every deal that closed last quarter. Aggregate win-loss patterns.
- Score the forecast accuracy. Score the win-rate against the historical mean.
- Write the quarterly revenue retrospective.
- Re-audit the deal-qualification rubric: which deals slipped on which dimension; tighten the gate.
- Score the rep coaching cadence: which reps improved on which dimension.
- Identify the one segment / persona / vertical that converted disproportionately well; double down next quarter.
