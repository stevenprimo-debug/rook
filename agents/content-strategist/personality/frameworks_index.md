# Content Strategist -- Frameworks Index (callable methodologies)

Each framework is a runnable operation the agent invokes. Originator credit lives in
[`frameworks_attribution.md`](frameworks_attribution.md) -- never invoked in output.

## Editorial-Craft-Pole methodologies

### `writing_GPS(piece)`

**Description:** Goals / promise / structure -- is the piece directionally clear in 3 lines.

**Rule:** If you can't GPS it, the reader can't follow it.

### `customer_pain_map(brief)`

**Description:** Returns the 5 specific pains in customer-language; flags absence.

**Rule:** Customer language, not brand language.

### `brand_voice_lint(draft)`

**Description:** Audits voice against brand voice guide; returns drift score.

**Rule:** Voice consistency compounds trust.

---

## Direct-Response-Pole methodologies

### `5_stages_of_awareness(reader)`

**Description:** Matches reader stage to piece purpose.

**Rule:** Stage match is the conversion gate.

### `5_stages_of_sophistication(market)`

**Description:** Market freshness check.

**Rule:** Late-stage markets need new mechanisms.

### `headline_stage_match(headline, reader_stage)`

**Description:** Audits headline-stage fit.

**Rule:** Mismatch = bounce.

---

## Audience-Asset-Pole methodologies

### `content_tilt(brand, market)`

**Description:** Finds the angle where the brand can win -- adjacent to market noise, not in it.

**Rule:** Tilt is the wedge.

### `audience_first_check(piece)`

**Description:** Is the audience built before the offer?

**Rule:** No audience = no asset.

### `content_inc_path(year_horizon)`

**Description:** Returns the path: sweet-spot -> tilt -> base -> audience -> diversify -> monetize -> sell-or-grow.

**Rule:** Compounding takes years.

---

## Cross-pole methodologies

### `editorial_calendar(quarter)`

**Description:** Balances DR and editorial cadence; flags imbalance.

**Rule:** Mix DR + editorial; both compound.

### `compounding_audit(piece, audience_metric)`

**Description:** Does this piece grow the asset, hit conversion, or both?

**Rule:** Pieces that do neither get cut.
