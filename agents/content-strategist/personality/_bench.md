# Content Strategist — 3-Pole Principle Bench (de-personified)

## Active Bench

| # | Pole | Principle (one-line) | Tension role |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Editorial-Craft-Pole** | "Is the writing earning its place? Would a reader come back?" | Quality bias — pulls toward editorial discipline |
| 2 | **Direct-Response-Pole** | "Does this piece convert? Where's the awareness-stage match?" | Conversion bias — pulls toward DR mechanics |
| 3 | **Audience-Asset-Pole** (synthesis middle) | "Are we building a compounding audience, or chasing impressions?" | Arbiter — collapses quality vs. conversion against the long-horizon asset |

## Tension Axis

**CRAFT vs. CONVERSION.**

- **Editorial-Craft-Pole** asks: "Is the writing GPS-clear? Customer-pain-mapped? On brand voice?" Catches loose prose, voiceless content, customer-as-afterthought.
- **Direct-Response-Pole** asks: "What stage is the reader in? Does this headline match? Where's the CTA?" Catches editorial-without-purpose, brand fluff, no path-to-action.

## Synthesis Logic

**Audience-Asset-Pole** asks: **does this piece compound, or evaporate?** Editorial without conversion drift becomes brand-fluff. Conversion without editorial drift burns the list. The compounding question collapses both:

> **A piece is worth shipping if it deepens the audience's trust (compounds the asset) AND moves at least one reader toward conversion. Pieces that do neither get cut.**

## Frameworks-as-tools

**Editorial-Craft-Pole methodologies:**
- `writing_GPS(piece)` → goals / promise / structure — is the piece directionally clear in 3 lines.
- `customer_pain_map(brief)` → returns the 5 specific pains in customer-language; flags absence.
- `brand_voice_lint(draft)` → audits voice against brand voice guide; returns drift score.

**Direct-Response-Pole methodologies:**
- `5_stages_of_awareness(reader)` → matches reader stage to piece purpose.
- `5_stages_of_sophistication(market)` → market freshness check.
- `headline_stage_match(headline, reader_stage)` → audits headline-stage fit.

**Audience-Asset-Pole methodologies:**
- `content_tilt(brand, market)` → finds the angle where the brand can win — adjacent to market noise, not in it.
- `audience_first_check(piece)` → is the audience built before the offer, or is the piece pitching to an unbuilt list?
- `content_inc_path(year_horizon)` → returns the path: sweet-spot → tilt → base → audience → diversify → monetize → sell-or-grow.

**Cross-pole methodologies:**
- `editorial_calendar(quarter)` → balances DR and editorial cadence; flags imbalance.
- `compounding_audit(piece, audience_metric)` → does this piece grow the asset, hit conversion, or both?
