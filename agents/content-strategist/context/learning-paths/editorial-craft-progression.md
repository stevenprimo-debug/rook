# Editorial Craft — Progression Path

## Who This Is For

The operator who owns a long-form content surface — blog, newsletter, podcast, white papers, cohort lessons, email sequences — and refuses to let the publishing cadence collapse to "whatever feels good this week." Founders, content marketers, course creators, agency editors, and solo-operators building owned-audience assets.

By the end of this path you should be able to:
- Architect a topical cluster (pillar-and-spoke).
- Run a calendar that compounds week-over-week.
- Measure what's working without lying to yourself.
- Recycle high-performers into multi-format yield.
- Convert audience to owned-list at scale.
- Develop a recognizable house style.

## Stage 1: Foundations (weeks 1-3)

**Goal:** Internalize pillar-and-spoke. Build your first editorial calendar. Publish three pieces that match a coherent voice.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Everybody Writes* — Ann Handley. The most-cited working manual for content writing in B2B; the cadence chapters are the keepers.
- *Content Mavericks* — Andrew Davis. The "loyalty loop" framework; not the cleanest book but the architectural ideas hold.
- *They Ask, You Answer* — Marcus Sheridan. The "answer-the-buyer's-question" inbound playbook.
- *Epic Content Marketing* — Joe Pulizzi. The Content Marketing Institute founder; the long-arc strategic frame.
- **Animalz blog** (animalz.co/blog) — the content agency that taught a generation of B2B content marketers. Free; archives go deep.
- **Margaret Magnarelli on content strategy** — formerly at HubSpot; LinkedIn writing.
- **Brian Dean's Backlinko** — case-study-driven content writing; SEO-tilted but the structural lessons transfer.

**Practice:**
- Pick one pillar topic. Map 10 spoke articles that support it. Each spoke is a distinct sub-question or angle; together they cover the pillar. The map is the cluster.
- Build a 90-day editorial calendar: weekly publishing cadence, three pieces in flight at any time (drafting / editing / scheduled), one pillar piece per month. Hold the cadence for the full 90 days.
- Develop a house style guide: voice rules, vocabulary you use, vocabulary you avoid, sentence rhythms, structural conventions (e.g., every article starts with a problem statement; every article ends with a one-sentence ask). The style guide is for you, not for anyone else.

**Skill check:**
- Your 90-day calendar is on time at week 12.
- Three published pieces are recognizably yours by voice alone.
- The pillar-and-spoke structure is visible on the site (internal links cluster correctly).
- You can defend each piece's job-to-be-done in one sentence.

## Stage 2: Applied Practice (weeks 4-10)

**Goal:** Run a measurement loop. Build email-list as the audience asset. Develop a recycling pipeline.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Building a StoryBrand* — Donald Miller. Useful frame for editorial that converts; ignore the cult.
- *Made to Stick* — Chip and Dan Heath. SUCCESS framework; the structural toolbox for memorable content.
- *Writing Without Bullshit* — Josh Bernoff. The "iron imperative" — your reader's time is more important than yours.
- *Newsletter Ninja* — Tammi Labrecque. Email-list-as-asset; the indie-author lens transfers entirely.
- **Convert Kit (now Kit) blog and case studies** — newsletter operator playbook.
- **The Tilt newsletter** — Joe Pulizzi's solo-content-entrepreneur newsletter.
- **Justin Welsh's content** (justinwelsh.me) — solo-creator newsletter and LinkedIn playbook at scale.
- **Pat Flynn's Smart Passive Income** — the long-arc creator-business archive.
- **Lenny Rachitsky's newsletter** as a study object — how a paid newsletter is structured and what the upgrade path looks like.

**Practice:**
- Set up email list on ConvertKit (now Kit), Beehiiv, or Substack. Build a lead magnet (downloadable PDF, email course, swipe file). Drive readers from every published piece to the list. Measure list growth weekly.
- Build a measurement dashboard: traffic per piece, time-on-page, scroll depth (if available), conversion-to-email, replies / shares. Pick three metrics that matter; ignore the rest.
- Develop a recycling pipeline: every long-form piece becomes (a) an email to the list, (b) a Twitter / X thread, (c) a LinkedIn post, (d) source material for a future pillar. The same content delivered four ways earns four impressions.
- Run a quarterly content audit: every published piece, traffic, conversions, dwell time. Keep, update, consolidate, or noindex. The audit produces the next 90 days.

**Skill check:**
- Your email list is growing weekly.
- Your top-three pieces by traffic are converting at a measured rate.
- Your recycling pipeline is producing 3-4 derivative pieces per pillar without you starting from scratch each time.
- Your quarterly content audit produced three concrete next-quarter decisions.

## Stage 3: Advanced Mastery (months 3-9)

**Goal:** Develop topical authority. Run a structured editorial team (even if it's you in different modes). Build paid product on the audience asset.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Content Strategy for the Web* — Kristina Halvorson. The web-content-strategy bible.
- *Designing Connected Content* — Carrie Hane and Mike Atherton. Structured content; the editorial-as-information-architecture lens.
- *Show Your Work* — Austin Kleon. The "publish the process" discipline; the audience that compounds is the one watching the process.
- *Steal Like an Artist* — Austin Kleon. The companion.
- *On Writing* — Stephen King. The most useful working-writer memoir-as-craft-book.
- *The War of Art* — Steven Pressfield. The professional vs. amateur distinction; the discipline of showing up daily.
- *Atomic Habits* — James Clear. Read for the cadence-and-systems chapters; Clear's newsletter is the largest in the world for a reason.
- **The Content Marketing Institute's annual report** — the industry-pulse benchmark.
- **Stratechery** — Ben Thompson; the editorial-as-business-asset case study.
- **The Slowdown podcast** and **Longform podcast** — interviews with editors and long-form writers; the craft conversation.

**Practice:**
- Build one cornerstone piece: 5,000+ words, definitive on a topic, designed to rank for years. Spend 40+ hours on it. The cornerstone is the trust-building asset.
- Run a publishing cadence at scale: weekly or twice-weekly long-form, plus a daily or weekly social rhythm. Hold the cadence for six months.
- Develop a paid offering tied to the audience: course, community, premium newsletter, paid research. Launch it. Measure conversion from free-audience to paid.
- Build a peer review network: 3-5 writers in adjacent niches. Exchange drafts monthly. Critique gets the work better; the network gets you cross-promotion.

**Skill check:**
- You can name three pieces from the past year that compound traffic month-over-month.
- Your email list has cleared a meaningful threshold (10,000+ depending on niche).
- Your paid offering has launched and produced revenue.
- You can name the next 12 months of pillars without flinching.
- Your one cornerstone piece is generating compounding traffic and serving as a trust-building referral.

**Quarterly cadence:**
- Pull your top 10 and bottom 10 pieces by performance. Read them cold. Identify the variables that distinguish them.
- Update the style guide and the calendar with the lesson.
- Re-audit the pillar-and-spoke architecture: which spokes have not been written; which pillars need a refresh.
- Score the email-list growth rate against the previous quarter; identify the lever that moved or didn't.
- Re-run the recycling pipeline audit: which derivative pieces outperformed their long-form source.
