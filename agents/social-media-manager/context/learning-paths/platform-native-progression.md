# Platform-Native — Progression Path

## Who This Is For

The operator running short-form social distribution — Twitter / X, LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Reels — and refusing to cross-post the same caption to every platform because it's easy. Founders, solo creators, agency operators, marketing leads.

By the end of this path you should be able to:
- Write a hook that earns the rest of the scroll.
- Hold a posting cadence that compounds.
- Master each platform's native format.
- Read analytics without confusing engagement-rate vanity with the metric that actually grows the audience.
- Convert social audience to owned-list at measured rate.
- Develop a voice that survives a blind test.

## Stage 1: Foundations (weeks 1-3)

**Goal:** Internalize hooks. Pick two platforms. Hold a daily cadence for 21 days.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Contagious* — Jonah Berger. The STEPPS framework for why content spreads. Read once for theory; pull the chapter on triggers for craft.
- *Hooked* — Nir Eyal. Behavior design; the variable-reward loop that powers feeds.
- *One Million Followers* — Brendan Kane. Practical short-form playbook; the case studies date but the structural lessons hold.
- **Alex Hormozi's $100M Leads book** (free PDF on his site) — the lead-generation lens applied to social.
- **Justin Welsh's "The Creator MBA"** materials (justinwelsh.me) — the solo-creator newsletter and social playbook.
- **Dickie Bush and Nicolas Cole's "Ship 30 for 30"** — the cohort that teaches daily-publishing discipline at scale.
- **The Generalist's Substack on Twitter / X growth** — Mario Gabriele.

**Practice:**
- Pick two platforms (not five). Match platforms to your audience: B2B SaaS = LinkedIn + Twitter; consumer = Instagram + TikTok; technical = Twitter + YouTube; creator = pick the one where your audience already lives.
- Write 50 hooks for one core idea you want to express. The first 30 are warmup. Aim for 10 that earn the rest of the scroll.
- Hold a daily posting cadence on one platform for 21 days. One post per day. The discipline matters more than the content.
- Build a swipe file of 200 high-performing posts from your platform. Tag by hook type, length, format, what makes them work. The swipe is your training set.

**Skill check:**
- Of 21 posts, at least three outperformed your baseline meaningfully.
- You can name what made them work in terms of hook structure or platform-native format — not "the algorithm liked it."
- Your 50-hook drill produced at least 10 hooks worth recycling.
- Your daily cadence held for the full 21 days without burnout.

## Stage 2: Applied Practice (weeks 4-10)

**Goal:** Master one platform's native format. Build cadence at scale. Measure what actually moves the audience.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Atomic Habits* — James Clear. The cadence-and-systems chapters; Clear's social discipline is its own case study.
- **The Tim Ferriss Show interviews with Naval Ravikant, Derek Sivers, and Seth Godin** — the long-arc creator-craft conversation.
- **Gary Vaynerchuk's books and content** — divisive style; the volume-and-cadence discipline is the keeper.
- **Jack Butcher's Visualize Value** — the design-meets-philosophy social pattern at scale.
- **Cole Schafer's writing on copy** (chasingdeadtime.com) — the hook-craft you steal from.
- **Sahil Bloom's Twitter and newsletter** — the thread structure that's been most-copied since 2020.
- *Steal Like an Artist* and *Show Your Work* — Austin Kleon. The "publish the process" discipline.
- **TikTok's Creator Academy** (free, tiktok.com/creator-academy) — even if you don't post on TikTok, the format-craft lessons transfer.
- **LinkedIn Creator Mode** documentation and the LinkedIn Editor program writers (Justin Welsh, Lara Acosta, Jasmin Alić).

**Practice:**
- Pick ONE platform to go deep. Study its native format: video aspect ratio, caption length, opening-line conventions, comment-bait patterns, hashtag norms (where they still matter), posting times. Match the format. Do not import another platform's format.
- Increase cadence to 1-3 posts per day on your primary platform for 60 days. Sustain it.
- Build an analytics-discipline: weekly review of top 3 and bottom 3 posts. Identify variables. The variables that distinguish top from bottom are your next 30 days of posts.
- Develop a content-pillar system: 3-5 themes you post under. Every post is tagged to a pillar. The pillars build topical authority on the platform; random posting does not.
- Test one format experiment monthly: long-form thread vs. short-form, carousel vs. single image, video vs. text, voice-over vs. text-on-screen. The experiment teaches faster than the swipe file.

**Skill check:**
- Your follower count is growing month-over-month at a meaningful rate.
- Your top posts are clustered around 1-2 pillar themes.
- Your platform-native format choices match the platform — your video is shot vertically for vertical platforms, your threads are sized for the platform's reading rhythm.
- Your content-pillar system has been tested across 60 days of posts.

## Stage 3: Advanced Mastery (months 3-9)

**Goal:** Build cross-platform distribution from one core piece. Develop a recognizable voice. Convert audience to owned-list at scale.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Cumulative Advantage* — Mark Schaefer. The compounding-audience case for sustained cadence.
- *The Creator Economy Report* — annual; CreatorIQ and similar; useful pulse on the platform economics.
- **MrBeast's behind-the-scenes interviews and documents** — the long-form video discipline that compounds; even if you don't post video, the structural lessons matter.
- **Casey Neistat's vlogs and interviews on craft** — the long-arc creator-discipline.
- *Stop Reading the News* — Rolf Dobelli. The defensive case for what you consume affecting what you produce.
- **Marie Forleo, Amy Porterfield, Pat Flynn** — the creator-business archive at scale.
- **Hubspot's State of Marketing report** (annual; free) — for cadence and channel benchmarks.
- *Talk Like TED* — Carmine Gallo. Short-form presentation craft; the lessons compress to social.
- *The Tipping Point* — Malcolm Gladwell. Re-read for the connector / maven / salesman archetypes; your audience is some mix of these.

**Practice:**
- Build a one-piece-many-formats pipeline: one long-form piece (blog, podcast, YouTube video) becomes the source for 5-10 short-form posts across two platforms. Run the pipeline weekly for one quarter.
- Convert social audience to owned audience: pick the conversion mechanism (email list, community, paid product). Drive every post toward it. Measure the conversion rate. Optimize.
- Develop a personal voice document: three words your voice IS, three it IS NOT, sentence rhythms, vocabulary, three example posts in the voice. Pressure-test by having a ghostwriter write five posts in your voice; if they pass for yours, the voice doc works.
- Run a paid-audience experiment: a one-month paid promotion (ads, sponsorship, creator collab). Measure delta on audience growth, owned-list conversion, paid-product revenue.

**Skill check:**
- Your audience is converting to owned-list at a measured rate.
- Your voice is recognizable in a lineup.
- Your one-to-many pipeline produces 5-10 short-form posts per long-form piece without you starting from scratch each time.
- A ghostwriter could write in your voice and pass for you using your voice doc.

## Ongoing Development

**Stay current:**
- **Justin Welsh's newsletter** — solo-creator playbook.
- **The Tilt** — Joe Pulizzi.
- **Creator Spotlight** — newsletter on creator economics.
- **Marketing Brew** — daily marketing news; pick one daily-news outlet.
- **Modern Retail and Digiday** — for platform-news pulse.
- Each platform's official creator newsletter or program (TikTok Creator Newsletter, LinkedIn Creator Newsletter, Instagram Creator Updates).

**Communities to join:**
- **Ship 30 for 30** — Dickie Bush / Nicolas Cole's cohort.
- **Creator Science / Justin Welsh's community** — paid; solo-creator focused.
- **Foster** — premium writing community.
- A small peer-creator circle (3-5 creators) — weekly swap of top-performers + post-mortem on flops.

**Quarterly cadence:**
- Pull your top 20 posts by performance. Pull the bottom 20. Read them. Identify the variables.
- Write next quarter's content-pillar system from what you learned, not from what you wanted to post.
- Audit cadence: did you hold; if not, why; build the system that lets you hold next quarter.
- Score owned-list conversion: how many followers became email subscribers; what was the conversion lever.
- Audit voice: do recent posts sound like you, or like every other creator on the platform.
- Re-read one foundational text (Hormozi, Berger, Welsh, Bush+Cole); identify what you can use now.
