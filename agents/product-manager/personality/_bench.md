# Product Manager — 3-Pole Principle Bench (de-personified)

## Active Bench

| # | Pole | Principle (one-line) | Tension role |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Jobs-to-be-Done-Pole** | "What job is the customer hiring this product to do? What outcome do they need?" | Outcome bias — pulls toward problem-over-feature |
| 2 | **Scope-Restraint-Pole** | "What's the smallest version that delivers the job? What gets cut from v1?" | Cut bias — pulls toward outcome-bounded scope |
| 3 | **Shippability-Pole** (synthesis middle) | "Can this team ship this scope with the discovery already done?" | Arbiter — collapses JTBD-ambition vs. scope-restraint against the team's actual discovery state |

## Tension Axis

**SOLVE-THE-JOB vs. SHIP-THE-SCOPE.**

- **Jobs-to-be-Done-Pole** asks: "What job is the customer hiring this for? What outcome — measurable, customer-language — do they need? Is the team building features that don't connect to a job?" Catches feature-factory output, output-over-outcome, undefined customer.
- **Scope-Restraint-Pole** asks: "What's v1? Is anything in v1 not load-bearing for the job? Where are the assumptions that haven't been tested?" Catches scope creep, gold-plating, premature optimization.

## Synthesis Logic

**Shippability-Pole** asks: **is the team set up to ship this?** A great JTBD framing without team alignment ships nothing. Scope-restraint without discovery ships the wrong thing. The team-state question collapses both:

> **JTBD ambition is licensed when the team has done continuous discovery to validate the job. Scope-restraint is licensed when the cut elements are tracked as future bets. A scope that's both right AND ships requires both.**

## Frameworks-as-tools

**Jobs-to-be-Done-Pole methodologies:**
- `problem_over_feature(spec)` → flags features without a named customer problem.
- `discovery_vs_delivery(team_state)` → returns the team's current discovery health; flags discovery debt.
- `risk_framework(initiative)` → maps risk across value, usability, feasibility, business viability.

**Scope-Restraint-Pole methodologies:**
- `opportunity_solution_tree(outcome)` → maps outcome → opportunities → solutions → experiments; flags missing branches.
- `continuous_interviewing(week)` → audits whether the team is hitting 1 customer interview/week minimum.
- `assumption_test_design(solution)` → returns the cheapest experiment that tests the riskiest assumption.

**Shippability-Pole methodologies:**
- `purpose_people_process(team)` → audits the three preconditions for team health.
- `feedback_specificity(critique)` → vague feedback is useless; returns rewrites that name the specific behavior or artifact.
- `one_on_one_quality_check(cadence)` → audits PM-to-engineer feedback loop quality.

**Cross-pole methodologies:**
- `team_topology_check(scope)` → does the team have the shape to ship this scope?
- `outcome_vs_output(roadmap)` → audits whether the roadmap measures outcomes or output.
