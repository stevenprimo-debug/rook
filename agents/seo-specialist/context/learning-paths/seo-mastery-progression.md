# SEO Mastery — Progression Path

## Who This Is For

The operator who owns a site whose distribution depends on organic search visibility — classical Google rank AND modern answer-engine citations (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini). Founders, content marketers, agency operators, and indie product owners who refuse to outsource the discipline to a black-box agency.

By the end of this path you should be able to:
- Fix a site's technical health to zero crawl errors.
- Write on-page that earns rank on real queries.
- Structure topical authority that compounds.
- Verify whether AI search engines are recommending your brand.
- Run programmatic SEO at scale.
- Defend an SEO strategy with unit economics, not rankings vanity.

## Stage 1: Foundations (weeks 1-3)

**Goal:** Speak the SEO vocabulary. Audit a site for technical health. Write one piece of on-page that ranks for a real query.

**Read / Watch:**
- *The Art of SEO* — Eric Enge, Stephan Spencer, Jessie Stricchiola. The 700-page textbook. Read it once cover-to-cover; reference it forever.
- **Ahrefs Academy** (ahrefs.com/academy) — free courses. The Sam Oh-taught beginner and intermediate courses are the best free SEO training online.
- **Google Search Central documentation** (developers.google.com/search) — the canonical source. The webmaster guidelines, structured data docs, and crawling and indexing sections are mandatory.
- *SEO 2024* or current-year — Adam Clarke. Annually updated; the no-fluff practical handbook.
- **Brian Dean's Backlinko blog** (backlinko.com) — the case-study-driven SEO writing that taught a generation.
- **Aleyda Solis's free SEO courses and her LearningSEO.io site** — free, ruthlessly curated, sequenced.

**Practice:**
- Run a Screaming Frog (free up to 500 URLs) or Sitebulb crawl on your own site. Find every broken link, every redirect chain, every missing title tag, every missing meta description. Fix them.
- Set up Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools. Verify both. Submit sitemap. Watch the indexing reports for two weeks.
- Pick one keyword with low difficulty and write one on-page article (1,500-2,500 words) targeting it. Use the SERP itself as the brief: what does the top result include, what does it miss, what query intent is the user actually behind.
- Add structured data (FAQ schema, Article schema, or Product schema as applicable) to one page using Google's structured-data markup helper. Validate with the Rich Results Test.

**Skill check:**
- Your site has zero crawl errors in Search Console.
- Your one published article ranks on page 1 or 2 of Google for the target keyword within 90 days.
- Your structured data validates in Rich Results Test.
- You can read a SERP and infer query intent without thinking.

## Stage 2: Applied Practice (weeks 4-10)

**Goal:** Build a topical-authority cluster. Earn organic backlinks. Run a recurring content + measurement loop.

**Read / Watch:**
- *They Ask, You Answer* — Marcus Sheridan. The B2B / service-business inbound playbook; the "ask buyers what they Google" discipline is the keeper.
- **Aleyda Solis's "Crawling Mondays" YouTube series** — short, technical, weekly.
- **Ahrefs YouTube channel** — Sam Oh and Patrick Stox. The deepest free SEO video archive currently.
- **Search Engine Roundtable** (seroundtable.com) — Barry Schwartz's daily SEO news; the canonical pulse of the industry.
- **Search Engine Journal** and **Search Engine Land** — daily SEO news; pick one to follow.
- *SEO Workbook* — Brian Dean. Backlinko's worked-exercise companion.
- The vendored reference clipping `context/references/` (when populated) for site-specific SEO patterns.

**Practice:**
- Build one topical-authority cluster: one pillar page (3,000+ words) + 5-10 supporting articles linking to the pillar and to each other. Map the internal-link structure before writing. Ship it over four weeks.
- Run a backlink outreach cadence: 20 contextual outreach emails per week for four weeks. Target real publications in your niche with one specific pitch. Track replies and live links.
- Set up rank tracking (Ahrefs, Semrush, or the free SerpRobot for small lists). Track 50 keywords weekly. Build a dashboard.
- Run a quarterly content audit: every URL on the site, traffic, ranking position, conversions. Decide for each: keep, update, consolidate, or noindex. The audit produces a 90-day content roadmap.

**Skill check:**
- Your topical cluster is ranking for the head term AND the supporting long-tail terms.
- Your earned backlink count is climbing.
- Your organic traffic is up quarter-over-quarter even without paid promotion.
- Your rank tracker shows movement on the keywords that matter, not just the easy ones.

## Stage 3: Advanced Mastery (months 3-9)

**Goal:** Operate AEO / GEO visibility. Build technical-SEO depth. Run programmatic SEO at scale.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Programmatic SEO* — content and case studies from **Lars Lofgren** (lars-lofgren.com), **Eli Schwartz's *Product-Led SEO***, and **Tony Chopp / Bernard Huang** content on the technique.
- **Bernard Huang's Clearscope and Animalz content** — programmatic and topical-authority work at scale.
- *Product-Led SEO* — Eli Schwartz. The strategic-SEO book most missing from the mid-career SEO reading list.
- **Andrew Charlton on AEO** and **Lily Ray's AEO research** — published case studies on appearing in AI search citations.
- **Rand Fishkin's SparkToro blog and Fishkin's writing on audience research** — the post-SEO discipline of understanding which sources your buyers actually trust.
- **The Search Off the Record podcast** — Google's own search team; the canonical pulse from inside Google.
- **Marie Haynes Consulting blog** — algorithm updates and E-E-A-T analysis at depth.
- *The SEO Toolkit* — Will Critchlow's writing at Distilled and SearchPilot; technical-SEO testing rigor.
- **JR Oakes's writing on Python + SEO** — for the technical SEO + scripting overlap.

**Practice:**
- Set up AEO tracking: pick 20 queries your buyers would ask ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini. Run each query monthly. Log whether your brand appears, how it is described, and what competitors appear instead. Optimize your content to earn citations.
- Build one programmatic SEO surface: a templated set of pages (100-1,000 URLs) targeting a long-tail pattern (location + service, comparison, integration). Ship with proper internal linking, schema, and crawl-budget management.
- Run a Core Web Vitals audit and ship the fixes: Largest Contentful Paint, Cumulative Layout Shift, Interaction to Next Paint. Use PageSpeed Insights, Chrome User Experience Report, and field data.
- Develop an E-E-A-T (experience, expertise, authoritativeness, trustworthiness) profile for your site: author bios, citations, original research, expert review. Audit each top-traffic page against the E-E-A-T criteria.

**Skill check:**
- Your brand appears in AI search engine citations for at least three of your target queries.
- Your programmatic surface is indexed and earning long-tail traffic.
- Your Core Web Vitals are green across all major pages.
- Your E-E-A-T profile holds up under a peer SEO consultant's audit.
- You can identify which content moves the AEO needle and which doesn't.

## Ongoing Development

**Stay current:**
- **Search Engine Roundtable** — Barry Schwartz; daily.
- **Search Engine Journal** and **Search Engine Land** — daily; pick one.
- **Ahrefs blog and YouTube** — weekly.
- **The Search Off the Record podcast** — Google's search team; irregular but high signal.
- **Marie Haynes Consulting newsletter** — algorithm-update analysis.
- **Aleyda Solis's #SEOFOMO newsletter** — weekly curation of the SEO week.
- **Lily Ray on Twitter/X** — algorithm-update and AEO commentary.

**Communities to join:**
- **Traffic Think Tank** (paid; Matt Barby, Ian Howells, Nick Eubanks). High-signal SEO community.
- **r/SEO** on Reddit — variable quality; useful for tool questions.
- **MozCon, BrightonSEO, SMX, Pubcon** — pick one annual conference; attend in person.
- **The Online Geniuses Slack** — large free SEO and digital-marketing community.
- **WTSFest community** — Women in Tech SEO.
- **DataForSEO and Sistrix newsletter communities** — for the data-side discipline.
- **The SEO Signals Lab Facebook group** — variable but high signal at times.
- A small peer SEO circle (3-5 specialists) — monthly audit swaps.

**Quarterly cadence:**
- Run a full site audit. Run an AEO citation audit.
- Review the rank tracker for movers and decliners.
- Write a one-page report on what changed and the three moves for next quarter.
- Audit Core Web Vitals against the previous quarter; identify the worst-performing page templates.
- Audit the backlink profile: new contextual inbounds, broken inbounds, toxic inbounds (disavow if needed).
- Audit the topical-authority cluster: which spokes need updates, which pillars need expansions.
