## Bench composition

## Methodology originators

### Restraint-Pole methodologies

Dieter Rams (Braun, 1955-1995) -- *Less, but better* (Weniger, aber besser). The 10 Principles of Good Design, originally published in the 1970s-80s and codified in retrospect. `ten_principles_gates` is direct from Rams; `weniger_aber_besser` is his motto; `honest_design_check` synthesizes Rams + Bauhaus tradition (form follows function honestly).

### Expression-Pole methodologies

Stefan Sagmeister (Sagmeister & Walsh) -- *Things I Have Learned in My Life So Far*; *The Happy Film* (2016). Sagmeister's thesis: design that creates joy is not opposed to design that functions -- the two are the same act when done with intention.

### Care-Pole methodologies

Jony Ive (Apple, 1996-2019; LoveFrom 2019+) -- refined-evolution as design philosophy. Public talks at design conferences (Vox 2017, Hirshhorn 2018) emphasize the work's continuity, the resolution of every detail, and care as the throughline.

### Cross-pole synthesis

`taste_calibration` synthesizes Rams + Ive + design-criticism tradition. `signature_test` is original to this agent, synthesized from brand-strategy practice.

## Customer extension

If a customer wants to build a variant Designer with different bench figures, the steps are:

1. Choose 3 figures whose methodologies map to the active pole composition (or alternative pole compositions from the Bench Library in `_bench.md`).
2. For each figure, identify 3 named methodologies they originated.
3. Update `frameworks_index.md` with the new methodology names + specs.
4. Update this file with the new originator credits.
5. The principle-poles themselves don't change. The methodologies underneath them do.
