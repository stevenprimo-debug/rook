# Brand Coherence Pressure Test

## What This Framework Is

Brand coherence pressure test is the recurring discipline of
auditing every marketing surface — current campaigns, recent
shipped work, in-flight drafts — against the brand spine, voice
spine, and audience-asset framework, and surfacing the specific
drifts that need correction. The framework holds that **brand
coherence erodes silently**: small drifts per surface compound
into noticeable fragmentation over months, and the only way to
catch drift before it compounds is structured audit.

The pressure test is not optional or annual. It runs at
multiple cadences:

- **Per-surface gate** — every surface passes the test before
  launch.
- **Weekly running audit** — surfaces shipped in the last
  week get reviewed for drift patterns.
- **Quarterly comprehensive audit** — full audit of all
  surfaces from the quarter; drift trends identified;
  remediation prioritized.
- **Annual brand review** — spine evolution candidates
  surfaced; locked patterns updated.

The test operates as a structured checklist across multiple
dimensions:

1. **Spine fidelity** — does the surface reinforce the
   underlying truth?
2. **Voice fidelity** — does the surface hold the brand voice
   per the voice spine?
3. **Visual fidelity** — does the visual treatment match brand
   standards?
4. **Audience fidelity** — does the surface speak to the
   intended audience in their actual language?
5. **Cross-surface consistency** — does the surface feel like
   the same brand the audience encountered elsewhere?

Each dimension has a specific pass/fail. Failures get flagged;
remediations get scheduled.

## Why It Matters For This Agent

Marketing Director's Coherence-Pole gates every marketing
decision on whether the brand coherence is preserved across
time and surfaces. The pole catches three specific failure
modes:

1. **Slow-drift fragmentation** — surfaces drift incrementally
   from the spine over time. Each drift small; cumulative drift
   significant. The brand at year-end looks different from the
   brand at year-start without anyone naming the change.

2. **Channel-specific voice drift** — different agents/contractors/
   contributors produce surfaces with channel-specific voices.
   LinkedIn voice differs from website voice; both differ from
   email voice. Audience perceives multiple brands.

3. **Campaign-specific drift** — campaigns "experiment" with
   new positioning, new visual treatments, new voice. The
   experiments don't roll back; they erode the spine.

## Core Concepts

### 1. The Per-Surface Gate

Every marketing surface passes the gate before launch:

**Spine fidelity check:**
- Does this surface reinforce the underlying truth?
- Does the language match the spine?
- Would the audience recognize this as part of the larger
  brand story?

**Voice fidelity check:**
- Per the voice spine, are voice pillars held?
- Any forbidden vocabulary present? (elegant, premium,
  delightful, magical, elevate, leverage as verb-filler,
  deep dive, as an AI, etc.)
- Register matches audience (executive-buyer vs.
  practitioner-to-practitioner vs. community)?

**Visual fidelity check:**
- Brand colors, typography, spacing match standards?
- Visual treatment serves the story (per the visual storyteller
  stack)?
- Anti-slop discipline applied (no generic AI-default
  aesthetics)?

**Audience fidelity check:**
- Does the surface speak to the intended audience in their
  language?
- Does it address their specific circumstance?
- Does it respect their trade context (per locked feedback:
  brand to the customer's trade)?

**Cross-surface consistency:**
- If the audience saw this and the recently-shipped surfaces
  together, would they perceive a single coherent brand?

Failures get returned for revision before launch.

### 2. The Weekly Running Audit

Each week, surfaces shipped in the previous 7 days get reviewed:

- Sample of social posts.
- All email campaigns.
- Any landing pages.
- Any blog/content publications.
- Any sales materials shipped.

The audit produces:
- Drift patterns observed.
- Specific surfaces with notable drift.
- Recommended corrections.

The weekly cadence catches drift before it compounds. Patterns
identified weekly inform per-surface gate criteria for the
next week.

### 3. The Quarterly Comprehensive Audit

Each quarter, the full surface universe gets audited:

- All campaigns from the quarter.
- All content shipped.
- All sales materials updated.
- All product page updates.
- All paid advertising creatives.

Output: drift trends across the quarter, segments where drift
concentrated, recommended remediations.

The comprehensive audit also surfaces brand-evolution candidates:
patterns that recur enough to suggest the spine itself needs
updating, not just enforcing.

### 4. The Drift-Pattern Recognition

Specific drift patterns recur:

**AI-default warmth drift:**
- "Excited to share," "Thrilled to announce," "We hope you
  love..."
- Drift cause: AI tools default to warmth; copywriters
  drift toward AI suggestions.
- Remediation: voice spine refresher; copywriter audit.

**Marketing-ese drift:**
- "Elevate your experience," "Transform your business,"
  "Next-generation solutions."
- Drift cause: industry conventions; competitor influence.
- Remediation: forbidden vocabulary enforcement.

**Vague-specificity drift:**
- "Many customers report significant improvements."
- Drift cause: lack of access to actual customer specifics.
- Remediation: customer-truth verification before drafting.

**Audience-mismatch drift:**
- Practitioner content drifting toward enterprise language;
  enterprise content drifting toward consumer warmth.
- Drift cause: same writer producing for multiple audiences
  without context-switching.
- Remediation: audience-specific brief discipline.

**Visual-slop drift:**
- Generic AI-illustration; over-rounded UI; decorative
  geometric flourishes.
- Drift cause: visual designers using AI tools without
  anti-slop discipline.
- Remediation: visual storyteller stack enforcement.

### 5. The Spine Evolution Decision

Sometimes drift is signal, not drift. The audience or context
may have shifted; the spine may need evolution.

The decision-point:
- **Drift to be corrected**: the spine is still right; the
  surface drifted; remediate.
- **Spine to evolve**: multiple surfaces drift in the same
  direction; the drift may reflect actual audience or context
  shift; consider spine update.

The spine evolution decision is the operator's lock, not the agent's
silent reconciliation. Per the compounding-append memory
pattern, spine updates are explicit, timestamped, justified.

### 6. The Cross-Agent Coherence Check

The brand portfolio has multiple agents producing surfaces:
copywriter, designer, social media manager, content
strategist, sales outreach. Each has its own voice/visual
discipline.

Cross-agent coherence:
- Are agents producing surfaces that align with each other?
- Do social posts from social media manager align with content
  from content strategist?
- Do landing pages from copywriter + designer align with
  outreach from sales outreach?

When cross-agent drift surfaces, the remediation is upstream
(spine clarification, voice spine refresher) rather than
per-agent (each agent treated separately).

### 7. The Locked-Constraint Enforcement

Specific locked constraints from memory get enforced in every
audit:

- **No constraint-aware in public marketing** — internal context only.
- **No [your employer] client names in public marketing** — client data
  publication rule.
- **No "boss" framing** — "team that works for you and grows
  with you."
- **"Cheers," sign-off on [your employer] outreach** — no name, no
  emojis.
- **No mono fonts in client proposals** — typography
  discipline.
- **No emojis in client emails** — executive-buyer register.
- **No text wrap on KPI cards/labels** — design discipline.
- **Brand to customer's trade** — trade-specific framing.

Each locked constraint is checked. Violations get flagged
regardless of how minor; locked means locked.

## Common Applications

**Pre-launch brand audit:**
A new cohort sales page is about to launch. The agent runs the
per-surface gate: spine fidelity (specific to [your product] spine),
voice fidelity (practitioner-to-practitioner), visual fidelity
(brand standards), audience fidelity ([your customer market]),
cross-surface consistency (matches recent [your product] surfaces).
Specific flags surface; remediation applied; page launches
coherent.

**Weekly social media audit:**
The agent samples 10 social posts from the prior week. Two
drift patterns emerge: one post drifted into AI-default warmth
("Thrilled to share..."); one post used forbidden vocabulary
("Elevate your career"). Both get flagged; copywriter gets
voice spine refresher; pattern logged for the social media
manager.

**Quarterly comprehensive audit:**
End of Q2, the agent runs comprehensive audit. Reviews all
shipped surfaces. Drift trends: increasing use of "AI-powered"
in product copy; visual treatment in paid ads drifting toward
generic SaaS aesthetic; LinkedIn voice becoming more
formal-corporate.

Output: spine refresh proposal (do we want to lean into "AI"
language or away from it?); visual standards refresh; voice
register check for LinkedIn channel.

**Locked-constraint violation catch:**
An [your employer] outreach .eml file slips through with an emoji in the
sign-off. The audit catches; the .eml gets corrected. Pattern
logged: outreach generator needs the no-emoji rule reinforced
in its template.

**Cross-agent coherence audit:**
The cohort launch generates surfaces from multiple agents:
landing page (copywriter + designer), email sequence
(copywriter), social posts (social media manager), sales
outreach to [your employer]-related prospects (sales outreach). The agent
audits cross-agent coherence: do all surfaces feel like the
same launch?

**Per locked memory: "Compounding-Append + Contradiction-
Surfacer."** Audit findings are appended to brand-coherence
log; contradictions (e.g., this audit said X, last audit said
not-X) get surfaced to the operator for lock.

**Per locked feedback: "Self-Improvement Loop."** Each audit
produces lessons. Patterns that recur across audits become
brand-coherence rules. Rules that aren't being violated may
get retired.

## Anti-patterns (when this framework is misapplied)

**Audit-without-remediation.** Running audits, generating
findings, never acting. The audit produces no value unless
findings get applied.

**Per-surface gate skipped.** Surfaces ship without the gate
applied. Drift compounds in real-time; weekly audit catches
patterns too late to fix the already-shipped surfaces.

**Drift-as-creativity excuse.** Justifying drift as
"experimentation" without explicit experiment design and
rollback plan. Real experiments are bounded; drift framed as
experimentation is just drift.

**Per locked feedback: "Compounding-Append + Contradiction-Surfacer."**
Audit findings get appended, not silently overwritten. Audit
history compounds.

**Spine evolution silent.** Updating the spine without explicit
lock decision. Brand changes without anyone naming the change.
Per locked memory pattern, evolution is explicit.

**Locked-constraint enforcement gaps.** Treating locked
constraints as guidelines, applying them inconsistently. Locked
means locked; consistent enforcement is the discipline.

**Per locked feedback: "Verify Project Status Before Speaking."**
Audit findings reflect actual surface state. Stale audit data
produces stale remediations.

**Per locked feedback: "Match Execution Mode."** Even fast
execution doesn't skip the audit. Compressed audit (key
checks) beats no audit.

**Channel-specific drift acceptance.** Allowing "LinkedIn is
just different" or "Twitter has its own conventions" to
fragment the brand voice. Conventions flex (per the
hook-cadence-platform-native framework), but the voice underneath
holds.

**Per locked feedback: "Filter — Personal-Tool Patterns vs
Agent-Team Patterns."** Audit findings must be team-readable.
Multiple agents produce surfaces; multiple agents read audit
output.

**Audit fatigue.** Treating the audit as overhead rather than
asset-building. The audit is what makes brand compound; the
brand that doesn't audit fragments and erodes.
