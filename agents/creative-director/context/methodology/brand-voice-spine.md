# Brand Voice Spine

## What This Framework Is

A brand voice spine is the single document that codifies how a
brand sounds — the specific vocabulary it uses, the vocabulary it
refuses, the cadence of its sentences, the register of its
address, the relationship it wants to have with the reader. The
spine is the upstream document every downstream surface (copy,
design, social media, sales outreach, product UI text) is gated
against.

Brand voice is not "tone." Tone shifts by context (formal in a
proposal, conversational in social media). Voice is the consistent
underlying register that persists across tone shifts — the
signature the audience recognizes whether they're reading an [your employer]
proposal cover, a the cohort email, or a [your physical/SaaS product] product
update.

A well-formed brand voice spine specifies:

1. **Voice pillars** — 3-5 named qualities the voice holds
   (e.g., "specific, confident, practitioner-to-practitioner,
   anti-warmth-default").
2. **The "we sound like / we don't sound like" matrix** — explicit
   examples of voice-correct and voice-incorrect language.
3. **The forbidden vocabulary list** — specific words the brand
   refuses to use (across all surfaces, no exceptions).
4. **Register by audience** — how the voice flexes between
   executive-buyer audience vs. practitioner audience vs.
   community audience.
5. **Anti-pattern recognition** — the specific failure modes the
   voice catches (AI-default warmth, marketing-ese, writer-voice
   indulgence, generic-LLM defaults).

The spine is enforced by Creative Director: every surface the
organization ships passes voice review against the spine. Surfaces
that drift get returned with specific language flagged for
revision.

## Why It Matters For This Agent

Creative Director's bench holds three principles: Voice-Coherence-Pole,
Story-Spine-Pole, and Anti-Default-Pole. The brand voice spine is
the implementation document for the first and third poles.

- **Voice-Coherence-Pole** asks: "Does this surface sound like the
  brand, or does it sound like the surface-author's preferences?"
  The spine is the test.

- **Anti-Default-Pole** asks: "Did this surface slip into AI-default
  warmth language?" The spine's forbidden-vocabulary list is the
  test.

For the operator's brand ecosystem — [your employer] client materials, [your product]
cohort and community communications, [your product line] product
language, social-media posts across multiple voices (the operator's
personal, [your employer], [your product]) — the spine prevents drift across
surfaces and across time. Without it, each surface drifts toward
the surface-author's voice or toward AI-default neutrality, and
the brand sounds different in every channel.

## Core Concepts

### 1. Voice Pillars

Voice pillars are 3-5 named qualities the brand voice holds. Each
pillar is specific enough to act on, not so abstract that it
becomes filler.

For [your product] (the practitioner voice):
- **Specific** — uses concrete numbers, named scenarios, actual
  customer language. Refuses generalization.
- **Practitioner-to-practitioner** — the writer is in the
  audience's industry, not outside selling to them.
- **Direct** — no preamble, no warmup. The first line is the
  point.
- **Anti-warmth-default** — refuses "delighted to share,"
  "thrilled to announce," "we're so excited" defaults.
- **Honest about stakes** — names the cost of inaction without
  manufactured agitation.

For [your employer] executive-buyer audience (proposal/outreach voice):
- **Executive register** — speaks to decision-makers as peers,
  not as customers being courted.
- **Confident** — does not hedge, does not over-qualify, does
  not soften with "perhaps" or "I wanted to reach out because."
- **Specific** — names the facility, names the named space,
  names the named outcome.
- **No marketing-ese** — refuses "transformative experiences,"
  "elevate your space," "next-generation solutions."

The pillars are different per voice context — but each voice has
its pillars locked.

### 2. The "We Sound Like / We Don't Sound Like" Matrix

For each voice, a side-by-side matrix shows specific examples of
voice-correct vs. voice-incorrect language. The matrix is the
operating document copywriters reference.

the cohort example:

| We Don't Sound Like | We Sound Like |
|---|---|
| "Unlock your creative potential with AI" | "The session musicians you played with last year are using Suno to clone their own sound. Here's how to stay ahead." |
| "Join our amazing community of creators" | "There's about 60 of us in the cohort Discord, mostly [your customer community] who pivoted in the last 18 months." |
| "Transform your career with cutting-edge tools" | "By week 6 you'll have shipped one tool people pay for. The math gets honest about whether the cohort worked." |
| "Don't miss out on this opportunity" | "Cohort 3 closes Friday at 6pm CT. After that, the next door opens in October." |

The matrix is referenced during copy drafting and copy review. New
voice-correct examples are added as they emerge; new voice-
incorrect examples are added when drift is caught.

### 3. The Forbidden Vocabulary List

Specific words the brand refuses, across all surfaces. Each word
on the list is there because it signals AI-default warmth,
marketing-ese, or writer-voice indulgence:

- **elegant** — generic praise that says nothing specific.
- **premium / luxury** — marketing-ese substitutes for "specific
  craft."
- **delightful / magical** — AI-default warmth signatures.
- **elevate** — verb-filler that doesn't name what changes.
- **leverage** (as verb-filler) — "leverage our expertise" =
  "use our skills"; the simpler word is honest.
- **deep dive** — generic phrase the reader has read 1000 times.
- **as an AI** — meta-commentary that the audience doesn't
  benefit from.
- **unlock** — overused metaphor that's lost meaning.
- **revolutionize / transform** — when nothing actually changes
  fundamentally.
- **next-generation** — temporal claim that's almost always
  false.
- **best-in-class / industry-leading** — unverifiable, generic.
- **synergy / synergize** — corporate filler.

The list grows as drift patterns are caught. Each addition is
specific and justified by a real surface that slipped.

### 4. Register by Audience

A brand has one voice but multiple registers. The register flexes
by audience context:

- **Executive-buyer register** ([your employer] proposal, CFO outreach): formal,
  confident, no contractions in formal documents, executive
  vocabulary, no emojis, no exclamation points.
- **Practitioner register** (the cohort, [your community],
  [your physical/SaaS product] updates): peer-to-peer, contractions natural, named
  scenarios, technical vocabulary respected.
- **Community register** (Discord, social media): conversational,
  closer to spoken language, but still anti-warmth-default.

Each register has its rules. The spine specifies which register
applies to which surface, and what register-specific rules apply.

### 5. Anti-Pattern Recognition

The spine names specific failure modes the voice catches:

- **AI-default warmth** — "I hope this email finds you well,"
  "Thanks so much for...!", "I'm thrilled to share...".
- **Marketing-ese** — "Drive results," "Maximize ROI," "Best-in-
  class solutions."
- **Writer-voice indulgence** — sentences that exist for the
  writer's pleasure, not the reader's benefit.
- **Generic-LLM defaults** — "It's worth noting that," "It's
  important to remember," "On the other hand."
- **Curse of knowledge** — assuming context the reader doesn't
  have.
- **Vague specificity** — sentences that sound specific but
  aren't ("many customers report significant improvements").

Each anti-pattern has examples and the corrective move. The
spine is the diagnostic manual.

### 6. The Voice Audit Mechanism

Every surface that ships passes voice audit before launch. The
audit:

1. Read the surface against the voice pillars — does it hold?
2. Scan for forbidden vocabulary — any hits get flagged.
3. Check register — does the surface match the audience register?
4. Scan for anti-patterns — any caught get flagged.
5. Read the "we sound like" matrix — is the surface's tone
   consistent with the matrix examples?

Failed audits return to author with specific flags. The audit is
not subjective — it is checklist-driven against the spine.

### 7. The Voice-Spine Update Cycle

The spine is a living document. As new surfaces ship, new drift
patterns emerge, new audiences are served, the spine is updated.
Update triggers:

- A surface ships and the audience response reveals voice drift.
- A new audience is added (e.g., expanding from cohort to
  enterprise customers).
- A new forbidden-vocabulary candidate is caught repeatedly.
- A new "we sound like" example earns a place in the matrix.

The spine is version-controlled (per locked feedback:
"Compounding-Append + Contradiction-Surfacer pattern" — versioned
append on memory updates). History is the moat.

## Common Applications

**Pre-launch voice audit on [your employer] proposal:**
The agent reads the proposal against the executive-buyer register
pillars. Flags: "We're committed to delivering transformative
experiences" → marketing-ese; "elevate your space" → forbidden
vocab; opening "I wanted to reach out because" → writer-warmup.
Returns to author with specific flags and "we sound like"
examples for revision.

**the cohort email audit:**
The agent reads the cohort email against the practitioner-to-
practitioner pillars. Flags: "We're so excited to have you in
our community!" → AI-default warmth; "Unlock your creative
potential" → forbidden vocab. Returns with rewrite suggestions
matching the "we sound like" matrix.

**[your physical/SaaS product] product update audit:**
The product update reads correctly for the touring-engineer
audience: specific feature names, specific use cases, peer-to-peer
register. The agent audits and confirms voice held. Surface
ships.

**Social-media post voice routing:**
A post goes to LinkedIn (closer to executive-buyer register) and
Twitter (closer to practitioner register). The agent prepares two
versions, each calibrated to its register. Both versions hold the
voice pillars; the register flexes.

**Forbidden-vocabulary detection in client email:**
Per locked feedback, the operator's standard sign-off is "Cheers" — no
name, no emojis. The agent confirms compliance on every [your employer]
.eml output, in addition to the brand-voice audit.

**Quarterly voice-spine review:**
The agent reviews surfaces shipped in the last quarter, identifies
drift patterns, surfaces them to the operator for spine update. New
forbidden words added, new "we sound like" matrix entries added.
Per locked memory pattern: versioned append, never silent rewrite.

## Anti-patterns (when this framework is misapplied)

**Spine as policy-by-fiat.** The spine that the team doesn't
internalize is the spine that doesn't survive contact with
real surfaces. The spine must be specific, justified, and used
in actual review — not filed and forgotten.

**One voice for all audiences.** A spine that doesn't flex by
register produces robotic copy across audiences. Executive-buyer
copy that uses cohort-practitioner contractions reads as
unprofessional; cohort copy that uses executive-buyer formality
reads as cold.

**Per locked feedback: forbidden vocabulary discipline.** The
agent enforces this without exceptions. "Just this one time"
slips compound.

**Voice updates without versioning.** Per locked memory pattern:
versioned-append, never silent rewrite. Voice-spine evolution is
tracked, not erased.

**Voice over substance.** A surface with voice-correct language
but no specific content fails. Voice is the carrier; substance
is the cargo. The spine doesn't substitute for specific
research, specific proof, specific customer language.

**Per locked feedback: "Execute, Don't Preamble."** Voice
discipline includes refusing preamble in every surface. The
first line is the point.

**The "creative" exception.** Designers, copywriters, and
managers occasionally push for spine-violating language because
"this campaign needs to be different." The Creative Director
refuses unless the violation is explicitly approved as a
brand-strategy decision (rare). Drift via individual exceptions
compounds; spine discipline does not.

**Per locked feedback: "Brand to the customer's trade."** The
spine respects the customer's trade context — operator-facing
surfaces lean into trade materiality, not generic startup
aesthetic.
