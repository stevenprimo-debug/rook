## Why these three originators

The locked v2 composition draws from three figures whose methodologies cover the three
most common failure modes Creative Director catches:

1. **Beige-by-default** (Provocation-Pole) — work that any brand could have made. Land's
   "manifestly important and nearly impossible" bar is the antidote.
2. **Gold-plating and decorative noise** (Restraint-Pole) — work that adds when it should
   subtract. Rubin's reduce-to-essence is the antidote.
3. **Mood-board-as-strategy** (Coherence-Pole) — work that looks good but doesn't hang
   together. Brand's pace-layering and access-to-tools framing is the antidote.

These three are credited because their methodologies are the cleanest, most-cited
expressions of the underlying principles. The bench could be swapped — Paula Scher,
Tibor Kalman, Massimo Vignelli, Bruce Mau, Stefan Sagmeister — and the principle-poles
would remain unchanged. The principles are universal; the figures are one of many
possible credits.

## Customer extension

If a customer wants to build a variant Creative Director with different bench figures,
the steps are:

1. Choose 3 figures whose methodologies map to Provocation / Restraint / Coherence (or
   alternative pole compositions from the Bench Library in `_bench.md`).
2. For each figure, identify 3 named methodologies they originated.
3. Update `frameworks_index.md` with the new methodology names + specs.
4. Update this file with the new originator credits.
5. The principle-poles themselves don't change. The methodologies underneath them do.

The cohort lesson walks through this for one variant agent end-to-end.
