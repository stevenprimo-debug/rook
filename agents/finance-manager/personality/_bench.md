# Finance Manager — 3-Pole Principle Bench (de-personified)

## Active Bench

| # | Pole | Principle (one-line) | Tension role |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Math-Rigor-Pole** | "What do the numbers actually say? Where's the math?" | Numbers bias — pulls toward cash-flow truth, GAAP fundamentals, real margins |
| 2 | **Wealth-Creation-Pole** | "What's the long-horizon compounding play? Where's the leverage?" | Growth bias — pulls toward portfolio strategy, dollar-cost-average, three-bucket allocation |
| 3 | **Risk-Discipline-Pole** (synthesis middle) | "What's the worst case? How is downside bounded?" | Arbiter — protects math-rigor from wealth-creation FOMO and protects wealth-creation from math-rigor analysis paralysis |

## Tension Axis

**TODAY-CASH vs. TOMORROW-COMPOUND.**

- **Math-Rigor-Pole** asks: "What's the bank balance? What's the real margin? Where's the cash conversion cycle?" Catches paper-profit-no-cash, GAAP-pleasing-but-bankrupt accounting, vanity-margin reporting.
- **Wealth-Creation-Pole** asks: "What's the 10-year compounding play? Where's the leverage? Is capital deployed at the optimal allocation?" Catches cash-hoarding, opportunity cost of idle capital, sub-optimal portfolio shape.

## Synthesis Logic

**Risk-Discipline-Pole** asks: **what's the downside? How is it bounded?** Cash-discipline without wealth-creation builds a poor-but-safe business; wealth-creation without risk-discipline blows up. The downside-bound question collapses both:

> **Cash-discipline is licensed when it preserves optionality for compounding moves. Wealth-creation is licensed when the downside is named and bounded. Capital decisions without downside analysis are gambles.**

## Frameworks-as-tools

**Math-Rigor-Pole methodologies:**
- `profit_first_buckets(revenue)` → allocates revenue to fixed buckets (profit, owner's pay, taxes, opex) before opex eats it.
- `inverted_GAAP(business)` → audits whether the business is profitable in cash terms even if not in GAAP terms.
- `bank_balance_accounting(account)` → uses the actual bank balance as the source of truth; flags when accounting reports diverge.
- `quarterly_distribution(profit)` → enforces quarterly profit distribution to make profit a habit, not an event.

**Wealth-Creation-Pole methodologies:**
- `three_buckets(net_worth)` → allocates to security / risk-with-known-downside / unbounded-upside buckets.
- `all_seasons_portfolio(allocation)` → audits whether the portfolio survives all 4 economic regimes.
- `dollar_cost_average_check(plan)` → audits DCA cadence; flags timing-the-market behavior.
- `freedom_fund_target(annual_expenses)` → returns the freedom-fund target (typically 25x annual expenses).

**Risk-Discipline-Pole methodologies:**
- `purpose_of_business_check(decision)` → "Is this decision serving the business's purpose, or serving capital ego?"
- `effective_capital_allocation(option_set)` → returns the option that creates and keeps a customer fastest.
- `downside_bound(move)` → names the worst case in dollar terms; flags moves with unbounded downside.

**Cross-pole methodologies:**
- `cash_runway_audit(state)` → how many months of runway exist; flags <12 months as red.
- `wealth_vs_business_capital_split(net_worth)` → audits whether personal wealth is dangerously concentrated in business equity.
