# Copy Craft — Progression Path

## Who This Is For

The operator who writes the words that ask for the click, the open, the trust, or the purchase. Founders, marketers, course creators, agency owners, and any solo operator whose business runs on a landing page, an email list, or a cold-outreach pipeline.

By the end of this path you should be able to:
- Write a subject line that earns the open.
- Write a headline that earns the scroll.
- Write a body that earns the trust.
- Write a CTA that earns the click.
- Know which variable to change when one of them stops working.
- Develop a recognizable voice that survives a blind test against three competitors.

## Stage 1: Foundations (weeks 1-2)

**Goal:** Internalize the direct-response canon. Write headlines, leads, and CTAs to template. Read enough proven copy that bad copy starts to feel obvious.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Tested Advertising Methods* — John Caples. The split-test bible. Every headline structure you will ever use is in chapter 2.
- *Ogilvy on Advertising* — David Ogilvy.
- *Confessions of an Advertising Man* — David Ogilvy. The mid-century memoir companion.
- *The Adweek Copywriting Handbook* — Joseph Sugarman. Each chapter is a "psychological trigger" with worked examples.
- *Breakthrough Advertising* — Eugene Schwartz (out of print; PDF circulates legally on some seller sites; expensive in hard copy). The market-sophistication chapter alone justifies the search.
- *Made to Stick* — Chip and Dan Heath. SUCCESS framework; the structural toolbox.
- *Scientific Advertising* — Claude Hopkins. 1923; 80 pages; still useful.
- *How to Write a Good Advertisement* — Victor Schwab. The 100 best-performing headlines compiled with commentary.
- **The "100 Greatest Headlines Ever Written" compilation** — circulated as a PDF; useful as a swipe-foundation.

**Practice:**
- Hand-copy ten Ogilvy long-form print ads word-for-word. Yes, by hand. The exercise teaches rhythm that reading alone does not.
- Pick five landing pages from products you respect (37signals, Stripe, Linear, ConvertKit, Notion). Write the headline-hierarchy of each: top headline, sub-headline, section headlines, CTA. Reverse-engineer the funnel.
- Write 50 headlines for one product (yours or fictional). Yes, 50. The first 20 are warmups; the last 10 are where the work starts.
- Rewrite three of your existing pieces of copy after reading Caples. Compare to the originals.
- Identify the five archetypal headline structures Caples names; write one variant of each for your product.
- Build a "swipe file v0" — start collecting screenshots of ads and copy that work on you. Tag by what makes them work.

**Skill check:**
- Show someone three of your headlines next to three from a competitor. They cannot tell which is yours — your work has reached competent-default.
- You can name the five Caples structures from memory and identify which structure any competitor's headline is using.
- You can explain why a headline works in terms of specificity, curiosity, self-interest, news, or proof — not "it just sounds good."

## Stage 2: Applied Practice (weeks 3-6)

**Goal:** Write and ship one piece of copy that earns a measured response. Build a swipe file. Develop a personal house style.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Cashvertising* — Drew Eric Whitman. The eight life-force desires framework.
- *Hey Whipple, Squeeze This* — Luke Sullivan. The agency-creative side of the craft.
- *On Writing Well* — William Zinsser. Not copywriting; non-fiction writing.
- *Bird by Bird* — Anne Lamott. For the discipline of shipping rough drafts.
- *Everybody Writes* — Ann Handley. The modern B2B-copy working manual.
- **Harry Dry's "Marketing Examples"** newsletter and site (marketingexamples.com). Free. Short, image-rich, ruthlessly tactical.
- **CopyHackers** — Joanna Wiebe's site and courses. The voice-of-customer method is the single most useful modern technique.
- **VeryGoodCopy** — Eddie Shleyner's micro-essays. One per morning is a copywriting workout.
- **Sam Parr and Shaan Puri's "My First Million" podcast** — the copy episodes; the marketing-pattern episodes more broadly.
- **The "Copywriting Course" by Neville Medhora** — paid; the email-copy modules are the keepers.

**Practice:**
- Build a swipe file. Tag every screenshot or copy block by: headline / open / proof / objection / CTA. Aim for 300 entries in the first month.
- Write one piece of long-form sales copy (1,200+ words) for a real product. Run it past three people in the target audience. Rewrite based on their objections.
- A/B test one subject line on one email send. Even if your list is small. The discipline matters more than the statistical power.
- Take three Joanna Wiebe customer reviews of any product on Amazon. Build a sales page using only the language from those reviews.
- Develop a personal house style v0: three words your voice IS, three it IS NOT, sentence rhythms.
- Read your own writing out loud. The places you stumble are the places to rewrite.

**Skill check:**
- Your shipped copy earned a measurable lift (open rate, click rate, conversion) over the previous version.
- You can name which variable did it.
- Your swipe file has cleared 200 entries.

## Stage 3: Advanced Mastery (months 2-6)

**Goal:** Develop a recognizable voice. Run a cadence-driven email program. Write a long-form piece that closes a high-ticket sale.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Influence* — Robert Cialdini. The six (now seven) principles of persuasion.
- *Pre-Suasion* — Cialdini's follow-up. The opening-frame discipline.
- *Building a StoryBrand* — Donald Miller. Useful structure; ignore the cult.
- *Words That Sell* — Richard Bayan. Thesaurus for direct-response writers; lives on the desk.
- *The Boron Letters* — Gary Halbert.
- *Predictably Irrational* — Dan Ariely. The anchor / decoy chapter is canon.
- *Thinking, Fast and Slow* — Daniel Kahneman. System 1 / System 2 underlies all copy that works.
- *Made You Look* — Carmine Gallo. Modern reframe of attention-craft.
- *Save the Cat!* — Blake Snyder. Screenwriting structure; the beat-sheet transfers to sales pages.
- **Eddie Shleyner's "VeryGoodCopy"** premium archive — paid; high signal.
- **Stefan Georgi's RMBC Method content** — direct-response copywriter; YouTube and articles.
- **Justin Goff's emails** (paid list; influential in the direct-response world).
- **Ray Edwards's content** — for the speak-then-write discipline.

**Practice:**
- Write an autoresponder sequence (7-14 emails) for a real product launch. Each email does one job: indoctrinate, build trust, handle objection, ask for sale.
- Write a 3,000+ word sales letter for a high-ticket offer ($1,000+). Run a peer critique. Iterate three times before sending.
- Run a 90-day publishing cadence on one platform (newsletter or social). Write three times per week. Audit what got traction; double down.
- Develop a personal house style guide: words you use, words you avoid, sentence rhythms, your three most-recurring metaphors.
- Build a peer-review network of 3-5 copywriters. Exchange drafts monthly.
- Run a one-month experiment where every piece of copy you write follows a different master's voice (Ogilvy, Halbert, Sugarman, Schwartz, Caples). The exercise teaches voice-as-choice.

**Skill check:**
- A reader can identify your copy in a lineup of three anonymous samples by voice alone.
- The work is no longer competent-default; it is yours.
- Your high-ticket sales letter has produced at least one closed sale at the target price.

## Ongoing Development

**Stay current:**
- **Harry Dry's Marketing Examples** newsletter — weekly; free.
- **VeryGoodCopy** newsletter — Eddie Shleyner, daily micro-essays.
- **CopyHackers blog and YouTube** — Joanna Wiebe.
- **The Copywriter Club Podcast** — Rob Marsh and Kira Hug.
- **Stefan Georgi's content** — direct-response copywriter; YouTube and his "RMBC Method" articles.
- **Ben Settle's email newsletter** — divisive style; the technique is sound; learn from him without becoming him.
- **Mark Ford / Michael Masterson's writing at AWAI** — direct-response old-guard; the lessons compound.
- **Justin Goff's daily email list** — paid; for the modern direct-response cadence.
- **Cole Schafer's "Honey Copy"** — for the brand-tilt-meets-direct-response voice.
- **Joanna Wiebe's "10x Landing Pages" workshop** — paid; high-signal.

**Communities to join:**
- **The Copywriter Club Facebook group** — large, mostly freelance copywriters; useful for craft critique.
- **Indie Hackers** — for landing-page critique on real products.
- **Online Writing Workshop** style critique groups — small accountability circles work better than big communities.
- **The Copywriting Course community** (Neville Medhora) — paid; high engagement.
- **AWAI's Barefoot Writer community** — for the direct-response freelance career track.
- A small peer copywriter circle (3-5 writers) — weekly draft swaps with structured critique.

**Quarterly cadence:**
- Pull every piece of copy you shipped last quarter. Read them cold. Rank by performance. Rewrite the bottom three from scratch using everything you now know.
- Audit your swipe file: prune what no longer earns its keep; tag what's still useful.
- Re-read one chapter of Schwartz or Caples; identify what you can use now that you couldn't six months ago.
- Score your sent email subject lines by open rate; identify the patterns of winners and losers.
