# Outreach Craft — Progression Path

## Who This Is For

The operator who writes cold messages — email, LinkedIn, voicemail, warm-handoff scripts — and refuses to confuse "I sent 200 emails" with "I had 20 real conversations." Founders, account executives, SDRs, agency operators, and any solo-operator whose pipeline depends on starting conversations from cold.

By the end of this path you should be able to:
- Write a first line that earns the read.
- Design a cadence that respects the prospect's time.
- Write a reply that advances the deal.
- Hold a quality-bar that scales without becoming spam.
- Personalize at scale without templating into the void.
- Trigger reversibility gates before every send.

## Stage 1: Foundations (weeks 1-3)

**Goal:** Internalize the first-line discipline. Write 10 cold emails that earn replies. Develop a personal cadence template.

**Read / Watch:**
- *Fanatical Prospecting* — Jeb Blount. The activity-discipline bible; the chapters on prospecting math and the law-of-replacement are mandatory.
- *New Sales. Simplified.* — Mike Weinberg. The hunter-discipline manual; pair with Blount.
- *Cold Calling Sucks (And That's Why It Works)* — Armand Farrokh and Nick Cegelski (30 Minutes to President's Club authors). The modern cold-calling playbook; the tactical detail is current.
- *Smart Calling* — Art Sobczak. The cold-call book that doesn't make you cringe; the chapters on the opener and the assertive value statement are canon.
- **30 Minutes to President's Club podcast** — daily tactics; the cold-outreach episodes are concentrated.
- **Josh Braun's content** (joshbraun.com) — the "no-pressure prospecting" framework; teaches that pressure is a tell.
- **Will Allred and Lavender's writing on cold email** (lavender.ai/blog) — the modern cold-email coach; data-driven.
- *Email Outreach Mastery* — Cole Schafer / others; pick one practical cold-email handbook.

**Practice:**
- Write 20 cold emails to 20 different prospects. Each email: under 75 words, one-line opener referencing something specific to the prospect, one-line value statement, one-line ask. Track replies.
- Develop a personal first-line library: 50 templates of first lines based on observable signal types (recent job change, recent funding, recent product launch, recent content the prospect published). Tag by signal type.
- Build a cadence template: email 1 → email 2 (5 days, different angle) → email 3 (5 days, value-add resource) → email 4 (10 days, breakup). Run the cadence on 20 prospects. Measure reply rate.
- Practice the LinkedIn DM equivalent: invite without note, then short DM after acceptance. The platform-native discipline matters.

**Skill check:**
- Of 20 cold emails, you earned at least 3-5 replies (positive or negative both count — silence is the failure).
- Your first-line library has been used 3+ times and at least one variant outperformed the others.
- Your cadence template is documented and reusable.
- You can defend each cold-email's first line with a specific signal you observed.

## Stage 2: Applied Practice (weeks 4-10)

**Goal:** Multi-channel cadence design. Personalization at scale. Reply-triage discipline.

**Read / Watch:**
- *The Sales Development Playbook* — Trish Bertuzzi. The full SDR-org operating manual; the cadence and team-structure chapters apply to solo operators too.
- *Outbound Sales, No Fluff* — Rex Biberston and Ryan Reisert. The post-Predictable-Revenue tactical update.
- *Cold Email Manifesto* — Alex Berman and Robert Indries. Short, practical; the multi-channel cadence chapters are useful.
- **Lavender.ai's content** — the email-coach platform's blog and YouTube; high-signal data on what's working in cold email right now.
- **Apollo.io Academy** (free) — Apollo's product-aligned training; the cadence and sequence content is genuinely useful even if you don't use Apollo.
- **Outreach.io's resource library** (free) — cadence templates and case studies.
- **The 30 MPC content library** — for the daily tactical bench.
- **Sam Nelson's "Agoge Sequence"** content on LinkedIn — multi-touch cadence design.
- The vendored reference clipping `context/references/hubspot-academy.md` and `hubspot-academy-1.md` for foundational sales-process discipline.

**Practice:**
- Design a multi-channel cadence: email + LinkedIn DM + phone + voicemail. 8-12 touches over 3-4 weeks. Vary the channel. Vary the angle. Vary the ask. Run on 30 prospects. Measure response rates by channel.
- Develop a personalization-at-scale system: snippets in your CRM or outreach tool keyed to signal types. The snippets feed the templates; the templates feed the cadence. The discipline is "specific to each prospect" without "from scratch for each prospect."
- Build a reply-triage matrix: positive reply → next-step playbook, "not now" reply → nurture cadence, objection reply → handle-and-advance, "remove me" reply → suppression list with audit trail. Each branch has a named next move.
- Run an A/B test on one variable in your cadence: subject line, opener, ask, CTA, send-time, send-day. Sample size large enough to matter. Document the winner.

**Skill check:**
- Your multi-channel cadence has produced at least 2-3 meetings booked.
- Your reply-rate by channel is measured.
- Your personalization system supports 50+ prospects per week without your quality bar collapsing.
- Your reply-triage matrix has handled every reply category correctly for one full month.

## Stage 3: Advanced Mastery (months 3-9)

**Goal:** Account-based outreach. Reversibility discipline at scale. Develop a high-signal voice that doesn't read like every other SDR's.

**Read / Watch:**
- *The New Strategic Selling* — Robert Miller, Stephen Heiman, Tad Tuleja. The account-strategy frame that informs multi-stakeholder outreach.
- *Eat Their Lunch* — Anthony Iannarino. The displacement-deal playbook; "winning customers from competition" is the discipline.
- *The Lost Art of Closing* — Anthony Iannarino. The "commitment-stack" model that converts cadence-driven meetings to closed deals.
- *Gap Selling* — Keenan. The discipline of "do not pitch until you understand the gap between current state and future state."
- *Influence* — Robert Cialdini. The persuasion-discipline that powers reply-rate-without-manipulation.
- *Pre-Suasion* — Cialdini's follow-up. The opening-frame discipline.
- *The Trusted Advisor* — David Maister. The trust-equation that converts cold to warm over multi-month cycles.
- **John Barrows' content and his Sell Better newsletter** — the outreach + selling craft.
- **Jen Allen-Knuth's content** (formerly at CEB / Challenger) — the modern challenger-outreach voice.
- *Spear Selling* — Jamie Shanks. The account-based prospecting frame for enterprise.
- **The Sales Engagement Lab** (newsletter from Outreach) — irregular but high signal.
- **Mark Suster's Both Sides of the Table** blog — VC perspective on outbound that founders need to internalize.

**Practice:**
- Run an account-based outreach campaign on 10 named target accounts. Multi-threaded (3-5 contacts per account). Custom angle per account. Six-week cadence. Measure: accounts engaged, meetings booked, pipeline created.
- Develop a reversibility-gate discipline: every send is a one-way door. Build a pre-send checklist (audience, list, message, suppression-check, opt-out). Run the checklist before every send.
- Develop a voice that doesn't read like every other SDR's: read your last 50 sent messages cold. Identify the verbal tics you share with every cadence template on the internet ("just bumping this up"; "circling back"; "found this interesting"). Remove them. Replace with what a peer would actually say.
- Build a quarterly outreach-program review: replies-to-sends ratio, meetings-to-replies ratio, pipeline-to-meetings ratio, won-to-pipeline ratio. The conversion-cascade tells you where to spend the next quarter's optimization.

**Skill check:**
- Your account-based campaign produced multi-threaded engagement on multiple named accounts.
- Your voice in cold messages is recognizable as yours, not as a template.
- Your reversibility-gate has fired on at least one send that would have been a mistake.
- Your conversion cascade (reply / meeting / pipeline / won) is measured and the weakest stage is named.

## Ongoing Development

**Stay current:**
- **30 Minutes to President's Club** — daily.
- **Josh Braun's newsletter** — weekly.
- **Lavender.ai's blog and YouTube** — weekly.
- **Sell Better with John Barrows** — irregular.
- **The 30 MPC LinkedIn presence** — daily snippets.
- **Apollo Academy and Outreach.io resource library** — quarterly refresh.
- **Sales Hacker** — daily.

**Communities to join:**
- **RevGenius** — sales and revenue community; free Slack.
- **Pavilion** — paid; sales-leader and IC community.
- **Sales Hacker community** — Slack and the surrounding events.
- A small peer-AE / SDR circle (3-5 reps) — weekly swap of best-performing first lines and biggest objection handles.

**Quarterly cadence:**
- Pull your sent / reply / meeting / pipeline / closed-won data.
- Score the conversion cascade. Identify the worst-converting step.
- Spend the next quarter optimizing that step.
- Audit your first-line library: which templates worked, which did not; retire the duds.
- Audit your reply-triage matrix against actual handling; fix the branches that misfired.
- Audit your voice in last quarter's sends: do they read as you, or as every other SDR.
